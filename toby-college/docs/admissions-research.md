# Admissions research — 2026–27 cycle (Fall 2027 entry)

Reference notes behind the workbook. All specifics come from official admissions /
financial-aid pages; **confirm anything dated once 2026–27 details post (~Aug 1).**
Applicant profile assumed throughout: very strong (1590 SAT / 38 ACT, full IB Diploma).

## Tiers (all 21 researched schools)

- **Ambitious:** MIT, Yale, Princeton, University of Chicago
- **Match:** Columbia, Brown, UPenn, Cornell, Swarthmore, NYU, Tufts, Williams
- **High confidence:** Wesleyan, Vassar, Oberlin, Connecticut College,
  University of Washington, RIT, University of Minnesota, Ithaca, Baruch (CUNY)

Approximate admit rates used for context (overall, not applicant-adjusted): MIT ~4.5%,
Yale ~4.6%, Princeton ~4%, UChicago ~5%, Columbia ~4.5%, Brown ~5.6%, UPenn ~5.4%,
Cornell ~7%, Swarthmore ~7.5%, NYU ~8%, Tufts ~10%, Williams ~10%, Wesleyan ~16%,
Vassar ~19%, Oberlin ~35%, Connecticut College ~37%, UW ~48%, RIT ~67%, Ithaca ~69%,
University of Minnesota ~75–80%, Baruch ~48–51% (median SAT ~1280, so a safety for
this profile; Zicklin business is more competitive).

## Early-round mechanics (the crux)

- **Restrictive SCEA:** Yale and Princeton — mutually exclusive with each other and
  with any other private early plan (including MIT EA, UChicago EA, and all EDs).
- **UChicago SSEN (ED0):** available to students who complete a UChicago pre-college
  program. Apply **Sept 1 – Oct 15**, decision **before Nov 1**; may reuse Summer
  Session teacher recs; counselor letter waivable. Binding. A deferral releases the
  commitment and still allows MIT EA (non-restrictive) + Regular everywhere.
- **Binding ED (one only):** UChicago ED I/II, UPenn, Columbia, Brown, Cornell,
  Swarthmore, Vassar, Wesleyan, Williams, Oberlin, Connecticut College, NYU, Tufts,
  RIT, Ithaca.
- **Non-binding EA:** MIT, UChicago EA, RIT EA, Ithaca EA, plus public schools
  (UW single Nov 15 deadline, University of Minnesota EA I/II, CUNY rolling).

## Testing

SAT/ACT **required** at MIT, Yale, Brown, Cornell, UPenn. Yale reinstated the
requirement 27 May 2026 (AP/IB no longer substitute). Everyone else test-optional
this cycle (Columbia optional now, required next cycle). Submit 1590/38 everywhere.

## Recommenders

Counselor + one math/science teacher + one humanities/language teacher covers MIT's
split requirement and every 2-teacher school. RIT needs one; UW and University of
Minnesota need none.

## Per-school essentials (shortlist of 10)

- **MIT** — own portal (not Common App). EA Nov 1 → mid-Dec, RA Jan 4 → mid-Mar.
  Essays 2026–27: 4 main (~100–200w) + 4 short responses (40–50w) + info box + 4
  activities. Recs: 2 teachers (one STEM, one humanities) + School Report. CSS+FAFSA.
- **Yale** — SCEA Nov 1 → mid-Dec, RD Jan 2 → by Apr 1. Supplement: academic-interest
  ≤200w, Why-Yale ≤125w, 4 short takes (≤200 char), one 400w essay (1 of 3).
- **University of Chicago** — SSEN Sep 1–Oct 15 → before Nov 1; EA/ED I ~Nov 1–3;
  ED II & RD ~Jan. Test-optional. No CSS (FAFSA + own worksheet). Essays: Why-UChicago
  300–600w + extended "Uncommon" essay 500–700w (five prompts change yearly).
- **UPenn** — ED Nov 1, RD Jan 5. SAT/ACT required. Apply to one of four schools.
  Essays: thank-you note + community + school-specific (all 150–200w).
- **Columbia** — ED Nov 1, RD Jan 1. Test-optional this cycle. Essays: list Q (100w)
  + five short essays (150w). Columbia College vs. Engineering.
- **Swarthmore** — ED I Nov 15, ED II/RD Jan 4. Test-optional. Two 250w essays
  (identity/community + curiosity).
- **Vassar** — ED I Nov 15, ED II ~Jan 14, RD ~Jan. Test-optional. One 300w essay
  (choose 1 of 2) + optional "Your Space."
- **Wesleyan** — ED I Nov 15, ED II/RD Jan 1. Test-optional. No required supplement
  (optional creative).
- **RIT** — ED I/EA Nov 1, ED II Jan 1, RD Jan 15. Test-optional. 1 rec, 1 supplement.
  ED I/II binding; EA non-binding.
- **University of Minnesota (Twin Cities)** — Common App or Golden Gopher app.
  EA I Nov 1 → by ~Jan 31, EA II Dec 1, RD Jan 1. Test-optional through Fall 2027.
  No recs (self-reported grades). One short essay (which of 8 colleges / 150+ majors,
  and why). Fee $55. Non-binding; no demonstrated interest.

## Other researched schools (in the tracker, not the shortlist)

Princeton (SCEA; graded written paper; short-answer trio), Brown (3 essays + 3 short
answers), Cornell (per-college essays; Engineering = 7 pieces), Williams / Oberlin /
Connecticut College (no required supplement), Tufts (2 short supplements), NYU
(Why-NYU ~400w), University of Washington (own essay + short response; ignores the
Common App essay; no recs), Ithaca (safety; 1 rec), Baruch/CUNY (one CUNY app up to
6 colleges; essay + 1 rec now required; Macaulay = separate app).

## Strategy notes

- Yield protection is real at selective LACs (Swarthmore, Vassar, Wesleyan) —
  demonstrated interest matters.
- Before any binding choice, run the school's Net Price Calculator so the commitment
  isn't blind on aid.
