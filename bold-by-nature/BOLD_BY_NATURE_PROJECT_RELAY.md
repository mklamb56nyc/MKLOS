## 2. RESEARCH — WHAT "GREAT" LOOKS LIKE (benchmark scan, preserved)

This research was done first, to define excellence before designing. **Primary benchmark = Dartmouth Outing Club.** Kept in detail because the user asked not to over-synthesize.

**Collegiate gold standard:**
- **Dartmouth Outing Club (DOC)** — the undisputed benchmark. Founded **1909** by Fred Harris; oldest & largest collegiate outing club in the U.S.; ~**1,500 members (~a quarter of the student body).** What makes it exceptional is the *ecosystem*:
  - **First-Year Trips** — student-run orientation open to ALL incoming students (optional but strongly encouraged; $420 in 2026 with aid-based reduction to $0), ~30 "Trip Types," no experience assumed, running since **1935**. It's the "front door" to Dartmouth. **[NUANCE 2026-07: previously stated as "for all incoming" without the optional/cost detail.]**
  - **Physical infrastructure** — 18 rustic cabins concentrated in New Hampshire **[CORRECTED 2026-07: previously "across New England"]**, maintains 50 miles of the Appalachian Trail, a lodge on Mt. Moosilauke, ~a dozen specialized sub-clubs.
  - **Student-driven governance** — the secret sauce. Kept student-led where peers (Cornell, Middlebury) institutionalized over liability concerns.
- **Williams / Colby / Bowdoin Outing Clubs** — show the model scales at smaller NE liberal-arts colleges. Common DNA: student leadership, low barriers, mix of casual outings + ambitious expeditions, deep ties to place.

**Boarding / high school programs:**
- **The Thacher School (Ojai, CA)** — possibly the single most distinctive HS outdoor program in America. All new students learn to ride/care for a horse (9th graders all year); riding test; horse-camping trip into the Sespe Wilderness; Gymkhana. Two wilderness base camps (Patton's Cabin in the Sespe; Golden Trout Wilderness School in the S. Sierra, 50+ yrs). Mandatory week-long backpacking for the whole community each fall & spring. Philosophy: *"The requirements involving horses and the outdoors are not peripheral, they are central."*
- **Colorado Rocky Mountain School (CRMS, Carbondale, CO)** — students spend up to 20 days/yr in the backcountry; ~2,000 hrs/yr volunteered to the U.S. Forest Service; pioneered the nation's first school kayaking program; Dewey + Kurt Hahn (Outward Bound) philosophy; 10-day Wilderness Orientation running since the 1970s. Alum: Conrad Anker.
- **Holderness School** — "Out Back," an outdoor education program woven into the academic curriculum (multi-day wilderness expeditions as coursework, not extracurricular).
- **Proctor Academy** — "Mountain Classroom," an off-campus semester traveling/studying across the American landscape; plus a fully integrated on-campus outdoor program.
- **Athenian School** — "AWE" (Athenian Wilderness Experience); phased independence model (instructor-led → student-led → solo → independent travel).
- **Storm King School (Cornwall-on-Hudson, NY)** — Outdoor Adventure program started as a club, expanded to year-round by demand; "survival points"; JV/Varsity/Captain ranks.
- **Phillips Exeter, Hotchkiss, NMH** — all have established outing clubs/outdoor programs (verified). **[CORRECTED 2026-07: Choate was previously in this list, but no outing club is verifiable on choate.edu — its flagship is the curricular Environmental Immersion Program at the Kohler Environmental Center. Drop Choate from peer-school pitch lists or cite that program instead. See `research/boarding-school-programs.md`.]**

**The five patterns that separate GREAT from merely good** (this is the analytical core):
1. **A signature experience** that defines the program (DOC First-Year Trips, Holderness Out Back, Proctor Mountain Classroom) — something everyone knows about, even non-participants.
2. **Student ownership** — student-led, not top-administered. That's where leadership development actually happens.
3. **Low barriers to entry** — no prior experience assumed; welcoming culture → broad participation, not niche.
4. **Their own language & traditions** — internal vocabulary creates belonging & cultural permanence (Trippees, Out Back, AWE).
5. **Connection to place** — rooted in the school's specific geography: *this* river, *these* hills, *this* landscape.

**Additional cross-cutting finding:** the clearest dividing line at the HS level is programs that are **mandatory & identity-defining** vs. a **club among many clubs**. The top tier (Thacher, CRMS, Proctor, Athenian, Holderness) all require every student to go through a significant wilderness experience. Second differentiator: **progression** (deliberately developing students from beginners into leaders over multiple years).

**All-girls positioning note:** for an all-girls institution, the outdoor program needs intentional differentiation from generic/co-ed models — position it through Porter's own language of boldness, power, and Sarah Porter's founding vision, rather than importing a generic "wilderness" identity.

## 4. THE NAME — "BOLD BY NATURE" (chosen; locked)

Went through an extensive naming process (dozens of options across four directions: place-based / Farmington River / Tunxis; Porter's-vocabulary names playing on Old Girls/New Girls/Ancients; mission-driven names on boldness/resourcefulness; and direct nature/adventure names). **"Bold by Nature" won because it threads every needle:**
- Echoes the mission ("informed, **bold**, resourceful").
- References the school's own "brave **in nature**" language.
- **Double meaning:** bold *by their nature* (inherently who they are) AND bold *by way of nature* (through the outdoors) — neither meaning overpowers the other.
- Avoids sounding like a traditional outdoors club (no "outing"/"hiking"/"wilderness" in it). "Sounds like something that belongs on a Porter's hoodie."
- Works as a standalone brand that can grow beyond a club into a program identity.
- Confident without being aggressive — right for Porter's.

**Explicitly rejected:** "Outdoors Club" / "Outing Club" — too generic, doesn't connect to mission or pillars.

**Candidate taglines discussed (not finalized):**
- *"Bold by Nature: Outdoor Leadership at Miss Porter's School"* (used as the subtitle on the one-pager)
- *"Bold by Nature: Where Porter's Girls Find Their Edge"*
- *"Bold by Nature: Adventure, Stewardship & Leadership on the Farmington and Beyond"*

## 7. THE SIX-PHASE PROJECT PLAN FOR MOLLY (her roadmap to execute)

- **Phase 1 — Crystallize the Vision (now → end of spring).** The one-pager is her calling card for every subsequent conversation. Leans on the Sarah Porter connection, the Farmington River asset, and the leadership angle.
- **Phase 2 — Find Her Faculty Champion.** Every club needs an adult sponsor; the right one is a force multiplier. Target an outdoorsy teacher or someone in sustainability/science who sees the curricular connection — ideally with institutional influence. One genuine conversation: *"I have this idea, can I show you what I'm thinking?"*
- **Phase 3 — Build a Founding Circle (not a big recruitment push).** Not 30 members — **3–5 genuinely excited friends** willing to co-create. Best move: **do something first** — organize one low-key outing (a Farmington hike, a sunset walk, even a picnic). The experience sells it better than any pitch. After that outing she has **co-founders, not sign-ups.**
- **Phase 4 — Go Through the Official Process.** Whatever Porter's requires to charter a club (application, student-life meeting, proposal) — submit with the one-pager, the advisor's name, and the founding group. Having already done a real outing gives credibility: *"we've already started, we just need the school's support to do it right."*
- **Phase 5 — Design One Signature Experience for Fall.** Not a whole-year calendar — one knockout event for **early fall / new-student orientation** of her sophomore year, giving Bold by Nature a visible identity from day one. A "First Outing" tradition for new girls could become the Porter's equivalent of Dartmouth's First-Year Trips.
- **Phase 6 — Build the Culture Layer.** The fun part / where Molly's personality shines — vocabulary, traditions, inside language. What do members call themselves? A tradition after every outing? A journal? A patch? Small things that turn a club into an institution.

## 8. CULTURE / VOCABULARY LAYER (ideas, not yet finalized)

- Best programs develop their own internal language (Dartmouth: "Trips"/"Trippees"; Athenian: "AWE," "Run-In"; Holderness: "Out Back"). Porter's already has **New Girls / Old Girls / Ancients** to build on.
- Bold by Nature should develop its own lexicon over time: names for the signature experiences, names for leadership roles within the program, post-outing rituals, a journal, a patch.
- **The endgame image (the north star):** a program so embedded that an **Ancient at her 25th reunion** says *"Remember that night on the river?"* — and every person in her class knows exactly what she means.
- Multi-year escalation vision:
  - *Year 1:* a small devoted following; build culture; name it intentionally "Porter's," not "Outing Club."
  - *Years 2–3:* a **"New Girl" outdoor orientation** experience for incoming students — the move that turns a club into a community institution (even one night camping on the riverbank creates shared class-wide memory).
  - *Years 3–5:* a **signature multi-day expedition** for a class year, with its own traditions/rituals — the version that rivals Athenian/Holderness and gives Porter's something no other elite girls' school has.
