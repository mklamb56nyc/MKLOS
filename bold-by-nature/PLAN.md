# Bold by Nature — plan & status

Molly's roadmap to execute, and where the project actually stands. Mike builds
the scaffolding; Molly is the face and driver. Everything designed to be run by
a motivated freshman/sophomore, not an adult.

## The six phases

- **Phase 1 — Crystallize the Vision (now → end of spring).** The one-pager is
  her calling card for every subsequent conversation. Leans on the Sarah Porter
  connection, the Wild & Scenic Farmington River, and the leadership angle.
- **Phase 2 — Find Her Faculty Champion.** Every club needs an adult sponsor;
  the right one is a force multiplier. Target an outdoorsy teacher or someone in
  sustainability/science who sees the curricular connection — ideally with
  institutional influence. One genuine conversation: *"I have this idea, can I
  show you what I'm thinking?"* (Research backs this: durable programs everywhere
  are student-led with a professional backstop.)
- **Phase 3 — Build a Founding Circle (not a big recruitment push).** Not 30
  members — **3–5 genuinely excited friends** willing to co-create. Best move:
  **do something first** — one low-key outing (a Farmington River hike, a sunset
  walk, a picnic). The experience sells it better than any pitch. After that
  outing she has **co-founders, not sign-ups.**
- **Phase 4 — Go Through the Official Process.** Whatever Porter's requires to
  charter a club — submit with the one-pager, the advisor's name, and the
  founding group. No public process is documented; frame as "per the Office of
  Student Life's process" and confirm internally. Having already done a real
  outing gives credibility: *"we've already started, we just need the school's
  support to do it right."*
- **Phase 5 — Design One Signature Experience for Fall.** Not a whole-year
  calendar — one knockout event for early fall / new-student orientation of her
  sophomore year. A "First Outing" tradition for New Girls could become the
  Porter's equivalent of Dartmouth's First-Year Trips. Logistics unlocked by
  research: CT DEEP youth-group camping permit ($33, up to 50 youth, school
  groups qualify, ≥14 days ahead, 1:10 adult ratio) at Peoples or American
  Legion State Forest.
- **Phase 6 — Build the Culture Layer.** Where Molly's personality shines —
  vocabulary, traditions, inside language (see `CULTURE.md`).

## Year-one seasonal program (from the one-pager)

| Season | Activities |
|---|---|
| Fall | New Girl Welcome Hike along the Farmington River · Apple picking & cider day · Sunrise yoga on campus · Leaf-peeping trail run in Talcott Mountain State Park |
| Winter | Snowshoeing in Peoples State Forest · Winter survival skills workshop · Stargazing night (astronomy, just like Sarah Porter taught) · Hot cocoa & campfire storytelling |
| Spring | Farmington River kayaking or canoeing · Trail cleanup & stewardship day · Overnight camping trip · End-of-year celebration cookout |

All venues verified viable in `research/farmington-area-assets.md` (Peoples SF
confirmed for snowshoeing; keep paddling upstream of Tariffville Gorge; Winding
Trails and Hill-Stead enable zero-transport outings).

## What we need to get started (from the one-pager)

- A faculty advisor who shares the vision (science, sustainability, or athletics)
- Official club status through the student organizations process
- A small budget for transportation and basic gear ($200–$500 to start)
- Five founding members ready to plan and lead the first outings
- "Your enthusiasm — come on one outing and see for yourself"
