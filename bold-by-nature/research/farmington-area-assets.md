## Farmington River — Wild & Scenic status

- The river carries **two** National Wild & Scenic designations. **Upper (West Branch), 1994:** 14 miles designated Aug 1994 from below Goodwin Dam (Hartland) to the Nepaug River confluence; +1.1 miles in Canton added Mar 2019 → 15.1 miles. (https://www.fws.gov/rivers/river/farmington-west-branch ; https://farmingtonriver.org/wild-and-scenic/)
- **Lower Farmington River & Salmon Brook, March 2019:** 61.7 miles designated a Partnership Wild & Scenic River after a 12-year community effort — designated towns include **Farmington itself** (plus Avon, Bloomfield, Burlington, Canton, East Granby, Granby, Hartland, Simsbury, Windsor). The river the club would hike and paddle beside is federally designated. (https://lowerfarmingtonriver.org/about/history-becoming-wild-scenic/ ; https://www.fws.gov/rivers/river/farmington ; https://www.nps.gov/articles/000/lower-farmington-salmon-brook-wild-and-scenic-rivers.htm)
- Stewardship: Farmington River Coordinating Committee (upper) and the Lower Farmington River & Salmon Brook Wild & Scenic Coordinating Committee. (https://farmingtonriver.org/ ; https://lowerfarmingtonriver.org/)

## Farmington River — paddling near campus

- Flatwater/quickwater from the **Route 4 bridge in Farmington** downstream ~14 miles to Curtiss Park, Simsbury — moving current, no rapids; access at Rt 4 (Farmington), Rt 185 (Simsbury), Curtiss Park. (https://www.simsbury-ct.gov/1063/Kayaking-Canoeing)
- The impounded section behind Collinsville Canoe & Kayak (Collinsville) is calm and flat, "perfect for beginners." (https://www.collinsvillecanoe.com/canoe-kayak-paddle-board---bike-rentals.htm)
- **Safety note:** whitewater at **Tariffville Gorge** (Simsbury) — Class II–III competition site; last safe take-out behind "The Mill" on Rt 189. Keep beginner trips upstream of Tariffville. (https://www.americanwhitewater.org/content/River/view/river-detail/443/main)

## River trails

- **Farmington Canal Heritage Trail (FCHT):** ~58 paved miles in CT, New Haven to the MA line (80+ total with MA), through Farmington on the old canal/rail corridor. (https://fchtrail.org/our-trails/)
- **Farmington River Trail (FRT):** 18.2 miles linking the FCHT at **Red Oak Hill Road, Farmington** to Hopmeadow St, Simsbury, via Unionville, Burlington, Canton/Collinsville — together a 28.5-mile five-town loop; ~6 miles closely follow the Wild & Scenic river (the most scenic section). (https://fchtrail.org/our-trails/)
- A wide paved riverside path begins on Meadow Road along the river's southern bank — roughly a mile from Miss Porter's (approx., UNVERIFIED). (https://www.cttrailfinder.com/trails/trail/farmington-river-trail)

## Talcott Mountain State Park (Heublein Tower)

- Summit Ridge Dr, Simsbury, off Rt 185; open 8 a.m.–sunset; parking free for CT vehicles ($10–15 out-of-state). ~8–9 road miles / ~15–20 min from Farmington (approx.). (https://ctparks.com/parks/talcott-mountain-state-park)
- **Tower Trail** (yellow): 1.25 miles each way, moderate, 30–40 min up — steep start then level ridge with vistas. (https://portal.ct.gov/DEEP/State-Parks/Parks/Talcott-Mountain-State-Park/Hiking)
- **Heublein Tower:** 165 ft, built 1914 on a ~1,000-ft promontory; museum/deck open Memorial Day weekend–Oct 31 (Fri–Mon 10–4); views claimed ~1,200 sq mi incl. Mt Monadnock, the Berkshires, Long Island Sound. Classic fall-foliage outing. (https://friendsofheubleintower.org/visit-heublein-tower/)

## Peoples State Forest (Barkhamsted)

- East River Rd/Rt 318, along the Wild & Scenic West Branch; 8 a.m.–sunset; CT vehicles park free. ~18–20 miles / ~30–35 min (approx.). (https://ctparks.com/parks/peoples-state-forest)
- ~11.2 miles of blue-blazed trails (Jessie Gerard, Agnes Bowen, Charles Pack, Robert Ross…); highlights: Chaugham's Lookout, Indian Caves/soapstone quarry, Barkhamsted Lighthouse historic site, CCC-built Stone Museum (Memorial Day–Labor Day). (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peoples_State_Forest_Trails)
- **Winter use confirmed:** snowshoeing on trails, XC skiing on portions — validates the relay's winter plan. (https://ctparks.com/parks/peoples-state-forest)
- **Youth group camping:** three sites for groups of 35–50, advance reservation. (https://ctparks.com/parks/peoples-state-forest)

## Other strong day-trip options (~45 min)

- **Rattlesnake Mountain & Pinnacle Rock (Farmington/Plainville), Metacomet/NET:** 2.6 mi out-and-back to 750-ft cliff views (400 ft gain), extendable to 4.6 mi incl. Pinnacle Rock; passes Will Warren's Den, a Farmington hermit's cave. Trailhead on Rt 6, ~3–4 mi from campus (approx.). (https://newenglandtrail.org/hike/rattlesnake-mountain-pinnacle-rock/)
- **New England National Scenic Trail (NET):** 215-mile Mattabesett-Metacomet-Monadnock route, National Scenic Trail since Mar 30, 2009, running from Long Island Sound at Guilford through the Farmington-area traprock ridges — multiple local sections. (https://www.nps.gov/places/new-england-national-scenic-trail.htm)
- **Hill-Stead Museum (35 Mountain Rd, Farmington):** 152-acre estate, ~3 miles of free walking trails (Bittersweet, Woodland, Swamp Oak), open daily, walking distance from school (approx.). (https://hillstead.org/visit-hillstead-2/)
- **West Hartford Reservoirs / MDC (1420 Farmington Ave):** ~3,000 acres, 30+ miles of trails around six reservoirs; sunrise–sunset; Metacomet Trail crosses it. ~5–6 mi / ~10–15 min (approx.). (https://themdc.org/reservoirs/)
- **Ragged Mountain Memorial Preserve (Berlin/Southington):** 761-ft traprock ridge, 6.5 mi of trails incl. 2.5 mi of Metacomet/NET; CT's most popular outdoor climbing crag (routes since the 1930s). ~10 mi / ~20 min (approx.). (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ragged_Mountain_(Connecticut))
- **Winding Trails (Farmington):** 350-acre private outdoor center in town — CT's only full-service cross-country ski center: 13 mi groomed trails, snowshoeing, rentals, ski school. ~2–3 mi from campus (approx.). (https://www.windingtrails.org/cross-country-ski)

## Land trusts & stewardship partners

- **Farmington Land Trust** (founded 1971): volunteer-led, 300+ acres across ~70 parcels in Farmington; volunteer **property steward** program (training provided, annual monitoring of an assigned preserve) plus events — a natural service partner. (https://www.farmingtonlandtrust.org/about-us ; https://www.farmingtonlandtrust.org/volunteer)
- **Farmington River Watershed Association (FRWA)** — 749 Hopmeadow St, Simsbury, (860) 658-4442; protects the river/watershed (founding year commonly given as 1953, UNVERIFIED); **annual river cleanup with 250+ volunteers**, invasive water-chestnut pulls, paddle trips, education. (https://frwa.org/)
- Watershed cleanups tie into the Connecticut River Conservancy's **Source-to-Sea Cleanup**. (https://www.trlandconservancy.org/event/farmington-river-cleanup/)

## Camping — the first overnight (~1 hr)

- **CT DEEP Youth Group Camping:** designated group areas at several state parks/forests — incl. **American Legion State Forest** and **Peoples State Forest** (three sites, 35–50 campers) — for nonprofit youth groups affiliated with a school. Permit **$33 for up to 50 youth, up to 3 nights**; written application to the DEEP district office ≥14 days ahead; adult ratio ≥1:10. (https://portal.ct.gov/DEEP/State-Parks/Camping/Youth-Group-Camping---CT-State-Parks-and-Forests)
- **Austin F. Hawes Memorial Campground, American Legion State Forest (Barkhamsted/Riverton):** 30 sites + six rustic two-room cabins (sleep 6) in pines beside the West Branch; season mid-April–~Labor Day; (860) 379-0922; ~20 mi / ~35 min (approx.). (https://ctparks.com/parks/american-legion-state-forest)
- **Hammonasset Beach State Park (Madison):** 550-site campground, Memorial Day–Columbus Day — doubles as the Long Island Sound destination. (https://ctparks.com/parks/hammonasset-beach-state-park)
- UNVERIFIED: AMC's Northwest Camp (Salisbury, CT) — distance and group booking terms not verified; likely at/beyond the 1-hour radius.

## Stargazing

- **Van Vleck Observatory, Wesleyan University (Middletown, ~20 mi / ~30 min approx.):** free public "Space Nights" Wednesdays 8 p.m. during the academic year — talk + telescope viewing on 16" and 20" telescopes incl. the historic 20" refractor. (https://www.wesleyan.edu/academics/departments/astronomy/observatory/observatory-events.html)
- **Astronomical Society of Greater Hartford:** monthly Saturday public observing at Van Vleck, always free. (https://asgh.org/public-observing/)
- UNVERIFIED: a true dark-sky site — Barkhamsted/Peoples is the darkest terrain regionally but has no designation and state forests close at sunset; an on-campus or Hill-Stead night session would need permission. (Nice tie-in either way: astronomy was in Sarah Porter's 1843 curriculum.)

## Long Island Sound

- **Hammonasset Beach State Park (Madison):** CT's largest shoreline park — 2-mile beach, Meigs Point Nature Center (touch tanks, live native animals), boardwalk, campground. Beach day + coastal ecology + overnight can be one trip. ~45–50 miles, ~55–60 min via I-91/Rt 9 (approx.). (https://ctparks.com/parks/hammonasset-beach-state-park ; https://www.meigspointnaturecenter.org/)
- **"Ridge-to-Sound" theme:** the NET's southern terminus reaches Long Island Sound at Guilford — bookends the club's home-turf NET ridge sections. (https://www.nps.gov/places/new-england-national-scenic-trail.htm)
