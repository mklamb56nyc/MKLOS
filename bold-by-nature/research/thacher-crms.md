# Fact base: The Thacher School & Colorado Rocky Mountain School

> The two "mandatory, identity-defining" benchmark programs. Researched and
> source-verified 2026-07-21. Verdicts are against the claims in
> `../BOLD_BY_NATURE_PROJECT_RELAY.md` §2.

## The Thacher School (Ojai, CA)

### Verified claims

1. **All new students learn to ride and care for a horse; 9th graders do this all year; there is a riding test.** — **CONFIRMED.** All ninth graders are paired with a horse in their first week and ride the entire school year, with daily care (mucking stalls before class, afternoon riding, evening feeding); "most have never ridden before." All *new* students must learn to ride — ninth graders for a full year, transfer students for one trimester — and "the major long-term goal on the horizon is passing the rider's test at the end of the fall season," which gates the move to independent, self-directed riding. (https://www.thacher.org/programs/horse ; https://catdc.org/why-the-horse-remains-central-to-the-thacher-experience/)

2. **Horse-camping trip into the Sespe Wilderness; a tradition called Gymkhana.** — **CONFIRMED.** Every freshman takes a weekend horse-packing trip (packing horses and burros with High Sierra packers, riding into the Sespe backcountry behind campus). Gymkhana: freshmen compete every Saturday in spring on one of three teams (Blue, Green, Orange), culminating in Big Gymkhana each May — barrel and pole racing, the rescue race, ring spearing, the Silver Dollar Pick-up — drawing hundreds of families and alumni. (https://www.thacher.org/programs/horse ; https://www.thacher.org/horse-program)

3. **Two wilderness base camps: Patton's Cabin (Sespe) and Golden Trout Wilderness School (southern Sierra, 50+ years).** — **CONFIRMED.** Patton's Cabin: a restored 1890s homesteader's cabin a day's ride over the ridge from campus (near the Sespe/Piedra Blanca creek confluence), a way station for backpackers and horse campers. Golden Trout Wilderness School: southern Sierra Nevada, operated by Thacher for over 50 years, historic log cabins and canvas tents. (https://www.thacher.org/programs/outdoor ; https://www.thacher.org/programs/outdoor/golden-trout-wilderness-school)

4. **Mandatory week-long trips for the whole community each fall and spring.** — **CONFIRMED (minor nuance).** Extra-Day Trips (EDTs): "Every fall and spring, the entire Thacher community takes off in small groups for a week of backpacking in wilderness areas like Yosemite, The Lost Coast, Zion National Park, the Redwoods, and the Sierra Nevada." One school source specifies six days. (https://www.thacher.org/programs/outdoor/extra-day-trips)

5. **Quote: "The requirements involving horses and the outdoors are not peripheral, they are central."** — **CONFIRMED.** Verbatim on the school's Outdoor Adventures & Challenges page, continuing: "The real growth at Thacher—and in life—comes from putting yourself in environments where you are not completely comfortable." Note: presented as a community/student voice rather than a formal mission statement. (https://www.thacher.org/programs/outdoor)

### Additional facts

- **Beginner-to-leader progression (camping):** students can earn "A- or B-level camper" status, qualifying them to plan and execute their own weekend backpacking trips **without faculty supervision** — a formal, credentialed independence ladder. (https://www.thacher.org/programs/outdoor/extra-day-trips)
- **Beginner-to-leader progression (horses):** freshmen move from fall riding lessons to designing their own training regimen after passing the rider's test; advanced pathways include Extreme Cowboy competition and an interscholastic Equestrian Team; Gymkhana teams are "led by seasoned upperclassmen riders." (https://www.thacher.org/programs/horse ; https://catdc.org/why-the-horse-remains-central-to-the-thacher-experience/)
- **Staffing:** each EDT group is led by an experienced faculty or staff member and deliberately mixes students from every grade; horse-packing trips are led by "seasoned High Sierra packers." (https://www.thacher.org/programs/outdoor/extra-day-trips ; https://www.thacher.org/programs/horse)
- **Onboarding by immersion:** new ninth graders go to the Golden Trout Wilderness School essentially immediately after arriving, accompanied by prefects and dorm heads — outdoor orientation doubles as community-building before academics start. (https://www.thacher.org/programs/outdoor)
- **Skills curriculum:** EDTs teach wilderness safety, map reading, food preparation, minimum-impact camping; other offerings include rock climbing, winter camping, sea/river kayaking, leveraging adjacency to Los Padres National Forest. (https://www.thacher.org/programs/outdoor)
- **Historical origin:** founded 1889; Sherman Thacher's horse requirement began partly practically (students rode five miles into Ojai daily for mail) and philosophically ("there's something about the outside of a horse...that's good for the inside of a boy"). Western riding required; English an upper-grade elective. As of 2023, freshmen ride during all athletic seasons; later entrants complete the requirement in one season. (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Thacher_School)

## Colorado Rocky Mountain School (Carbondale, CO)

### Verified claims

6. **Students spend up to 20 days per year in the backcountry.** — **CONFIRMED (spread across sources).** CRMS's GuideStar profile: "students spend up to 20 days each year in the backcountry" (Boarding School Review says 21). The school's own trip math supports it for new students: 10-day Wilderness Orientation + 5-day Fall Trip + 7-day Spring Trip. (https://www.guidestar.org/profile/84-0425174 ; https://www.boardingschoolreview.com/colorado-rocky-mountain-school-profile ; https://www.crms.org/programs/outdoor-program/)

7. **~2,000 hours/year volunteered to the U.S. Forest Service.** — **CONFIRMED (self-reported).** GuideStar profile: "volunteers an average of 2,000 hours to the U.S. Forest Service annually." Mechanism: the first three days of every Wilderness Orientation are a USFS trail-maintenance service project. School-reported figure; no independent USFS confirmation found. (https://www.guidestar.org/profile/84-0425174 ; https://www.crms.org/programs/outdoor-program/)

8. **Pioneered the nation's first school kayaking program.** — **CONFIRMED with wording caveat.** School materials say CRMS "was the first independent school with a kayaking program and produces top white-water paddlers." All traceable sources are school-derived — best stated as "first independent-school kayaking program." (https://www.guidestar.org/profile/84-0425174)

9. **Influenced by John Dewey and Kurt Hahn (Outward Bound).** — **CONFIRMED.** Founders John and Anne Holden (Putney School teachers) "drew strongly upon the ideas of Kurt Hahn and John Dewey"; wilderness trips became a substantial curricular element in 1971, drawing on experiential educators and the Outward Bound movement. (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colorado_Rocky_Mountain_School)

10. **10-day Wilderness Orientation since the 1970s.** — **CONFIRMED.** "A ten-day adventure in the surrounding high country for all new students," "the introductory experience at the school since the 1970's." Format: 3 days USFS trail service, then a week-long backpacking expedition in the Hunter-Fryingpan, Snowmass, and Maroon Bells areas; no prior experience required. (https://www.crms.org/programs/outdoor-program/)

11. **Conrad Anker is an alum.** — **CONFIRMED.** Listed among notable alumni; graduation year given as 1982. (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colorado_Rocky_Mountain_School ; https://www.privateschoolreview.com/colorado/famous-private-school-alumni)

### Additional facts

- **Staffing and credentials:** "All the expedition leaders are CRMS faculty members who are qualified, experienced, and certified outdoor educators," trained and certified in wilderness medicine — run by teaching faculty, not outsourced guides. (https://www.crms.org/programs/outdoor-program/)
- **External accreditation as risk management:** fully accredited by the Association for Experiential Education (AEE) since 2006 — a third-party program-quality and safety review a school program can pursue. (https://www.crms.org/programs/outdoor-program/)
- **Risk philosophy:** "When students are encouraged to take developmentally appropriate physical, social, and emotional risks, tremendous growth can happen." (https://www.crms.org/programs/outdoor-program/)
- **Student leadership pipeline:** Wilderness Assistant Volunteers (WAVs) — returning students apply in spring and are selected to help lead the next year's Wilderness Orientation; mentored by faculty while serving as role models. A concrete beginner-to-leader progression. (https://www.crms.org/community/leadership/)
- **Annual cadence:** Wilderness Orientation (10 days, new students), Fall Trip (5 days, early October), Spring Trip (7 days, late April), plus afternoon programs in kayaking, rock climbing, mountain biking, and alpine/nordic/telemark skiing. (https://www.crms.org/programs/outdoor-program/)
- **Program integration:** since 1953, a "balanced curriculum of academics, outdoor education, work projects, and community service" (~170 students; campus work crews also mandatory) — the outdoors is one leg of a four-part whole-school structure, not an add-on. (https://www.guidestar.org/profile/84-0425174 ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colorado_Rocky_Mountain_School)

### Sourcing caveats

- CRMS's "20 days," "2,000 hours," and "first kayaking program" claims all trace to school-authored materials; internally consistent with the published trip calendar but not independently verified. Day counts vary slightly by source (20 vs 21 vs ~22 for new students).
- Thacher claims are supported directly by thacher.org; the "not peripheral… central" line is official-page copy framed as community voice; EDT length appears as both "a week" and "six days."
