# Fact base: Miss Porter's School

> The most consequential file — these are the facts the pitch quotes to
> administrators, so wrong ones are embarrassing. Researched and source-verified
> 2026-07-21 against porters.org wherever possible.
>
> **Safe to quote verbatim:** the mission sentences, the "radically brave in
> nature" line, the athletics language, the 1843 curriculum list.
> **Do NOT quote:** campus acreage (unresolved), the Mountain Day favorite-
> tradition survey percentages (unverified), or "Saukiog vs Tunxis" tribe
> attribution beyond the safe phrasing below.

## Verified claims

**1. Mission statement — CONFIRMED.** Exact current wording (two sentences): *"Miss Porter's School educates young women to become informed, bold, resourceful and ethical global citizens. We expect our graduates to shape a changing world."* Note: "shape **a** changing world," framed as "We expect our graduates to…" — a separate sentence. (https://www.porters.org/ ; https://www.porters.org/mission/)

**2. "While Sarah Porter's vision was radically brave in nature, it was not accessible to all women." — CONFIRMED, verbatim.** On the Mission page, in a paragraph about the founder's legacy and the school's commitment to expanding inclusion/equity. (https://www.porters.org/mission/)

**3. Athletics language — CONFIRMED, verbatim.** *"Porter's athletics begins and ends with girls. Our student athletes learn to embrace challenges, set goals, take risks, and demonstrate their power on and off the field."* The page also emphasizes "teamwork, leadership, competition, confidence, and respect – while encouraging students to lead lives dedicated to fitness and well-being." (https://www.porters.org/athletics/)

**4. Founded 1843; sciences in the curriculum — CONFIRMED.** Homepage: *"In 1843, Sarah Porter founded a school."* Legacy page: *"Miss Porter's girls also learned arithmetic, trigonometry, history and geography, as well as chemistry, physiology, botany, geology and astronomy"* (alongside Latin, French, German, spelling, reading). (https://www.porters.org/ ; https://www.porters.org/legacy/)

**5. Skating, rowing, tennis; 1867 Tunxises — CONFIRMED with two cautions.**
- The school's own Legacy page: she *"required her students to exercise regularly — a notable idea for the time"* and *"encouraged participation in sports such as tennis, horseback riding, and baseball."* Skating/rowing are third-party-sourced (Connecticut History: "exercises (e.g., skating and rowing) and sports (e.g., tennis and baseball)"), not on porters.org. (https://www.porters.org/legacy/ ; https://connecticuthistory.org/miss-porters-school-in-farmington/)
- Tunxises: Wikipedia — *"In 1867, the school formed its own baseball team, the Tunxises, named after the Saukiog tribe who once settled the area."* **Caution:** Wikipedia says "Saukiog," but the school's own Legacy page says the **Tunxis** tribe originally inhabited the Farmington area. **Safe phrasing:** "the Tunxises, after the Tunxis people indigenous to Farmington." Don't cite the year 1867 to porters.org; it isn't on the current site. (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miss_Porter%27s_School)

**6. Mountain Day — CONFIRMED as a real, surprise-announced fall tradition; survey percentages UNVERIFIED.**
- Evidence: official school Facebook video captioned *"Guess what? Tomorrow is Mountain Day!"*; an official "It's Mountain Day" GIPHY sticker published by the school; Niche lists it among fall traditions. (https://www.facebook.com/MissPortersSchool/videos/257106316206828/ ; https://giphy.com/stickers/MissPortersSchool-transparent-its-mountain-day-630pAsvkUBNBWSd0GY)
- **No official description currently on porters.org** — the old traditions page 404s after a site redesign. Current Community Life page says only: *"Our traditions — timeless, reverent, and fun — are the backbone of our community. Girls find connections with one another experiencing what Ancients have before them."* (https://www.porters.org/community-life/)
- The 15% / "German" 21% / rings 20% survey: **UNVERIFIED** — not findable in Salmagundy (student paper, on Issuu) or anywhere indexed; may have lived on the deleted traditions page. **Do not quote those numbers.** (Replaces the relay §1 claim, which cited them as fact.)

**7. Campus acreage — UNRESOLVED; do not quote a number.**
- 40 acres: Wikipedia, Connecticut History. 55 acres: DRA Architects (the school's own master-plan firm: "55 acres… 72 buildings representing 460,000 square feet") and BoardingSchoolReview (typically school-supplied). Likely 55 = total property, 40 = core campus. **Recommendation:** say "the riverside campus in Farmington" and skip acreage, or confirm with the school. (https://www.draws.com/portfolio/miss-porters-school-master-plan/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miss_Porter%27s_School)

**8. Campus on the Farmington River — CONFIRMED.** School marketing copy: *"On the bank of the river in the charming village of Farmington since 1843."* Crew rows from the shared Porter's/Farmington Boathouse. (https://www.porters.org/athletics/crew/)

**9. New Girls / Old Girls / Ancients — CONFIRMED.** "Ancients" (= alumnae) used throughout porters.org (nav section "Ancients," "Ancients of Impact," etc.). New Girls/Old Girls documented on Wikipedia and in school news (rings presented to New Girls by their Old Girls at the end of ninth grade; "the German" is a New Girl tradition; each arriving student is greeted by her "old girl"). (https://www.porters.org/information-for-ancients/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miss_Porter%27s_School)

**10. Pillars and institutes — CORRECTED (partly).**
- The "Welcome" nav does contain Leadership, Legacy, Sustainability — but the **Leadership page is administrative** (head of school + trustees), not a thematic pillar. Legacy and Sustainability are genuine thematic pages. **Don't present "Leadership, Legacy, Sustainability" as a formal pillar framework** — the club's three pillars still map well to Porter's themes, just don't attribute a "pillars" framework to the school. (https://www.porters.org/leadership/ ; https://www.porters.org/legacy/ ; https://www.porters.org/sustainability/)
- Institutes, official names: **"The Institute of Technology, Innovation, and Entrepreneurship (TIE)"** (porters.org says "Institute **of**"; the affiliated pcgl.porters.org uses "Institute **for**") and the **"Institute for Global Education."** Umbrella: **Porter's Center for Global Leadership (PCGL)**. (https://www.porters.org/tie/ ; https://www.porters.org/global-education/ ; https://pcgl.porters.org/)

**11. New-club chartering process — UNVERIFIED; nothing public.** Porter's cites "over fifty active student-run clubs and organizations" (profile materials list 39 clubs + 5 alliances, incl. Earth Club, Debate, Model UN, Mock Trial, DECA, Concordia); the **Office of Student Life** organizes activities and club-sponsored events. No club-fair or charter procedure is published — frame as "per the Office of Student Life's process" and Molly confirms internally. (https://www.porters.org/community-life/)

**12. An existing student outdoors club — OWNER-PROVIDED (Mike, 2026-07-22); not findable online.** A small student outdoors club already exists at Porter's; per Mike it "really doesn't do much." Two targeted web searches (2026-07-22) found no trace of it — not in the school's public club listings, Niche/BSR profiles, or elsewhere. Name, membership, and leadership unknown. **Rule for all materials: never pitch as though there is nothing** — acknowledge it respectfully without naming or characterizing it beyond what we know. Molly should find out who runs it (see PLAN.md).

## Additional facts

- **Enrollment/mix:** ~350 students, grades 9–12, ~two-thirds boarding (2025–26 Wikipedia: ~350, 65/35; BSR: 345, 66%; school materials elsewhere: "334 students… from 27 states and 30 countries"). Use "approximately 350 students in grades 9–12, about two-thirds boarding." (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miss_Porter%27s_School)
- **Sports offered:** Badminton, Basketball, **Crew (fall and spring)**, Cross Country, **Equestrian**, Field Hockey, Golf, Lacrosse, **Ski**, Soccer, Softball, Squash, Swimming & Diving, Tennis, Track & Field, Ultimate, Volleyball. Crew and ski teams = existing outdoor-adjacent culture. (https://www.porters.org/athletics/)
- **Athletic facilities:** Oaklea Field (NCAA-regulation turf), Kiki's & Maple Fields, Cow Barn Field, Crisp Athletics Center, pool + squash courts, Gaines Dance Barn; crew uses the shared Porter's/Farmington Boathouse and hosts the Hayley Petit Cup. (https://www.porters.org/athletics/)
- **Sustainability hooks (strong pitch material):** roughly **three-quarters of campus is natural space**; native-plant landscaping and pollinator habitats; a **student-run campus apiary**; **Earth Club**; solar + LED retrofits; CFO quote about stewarding existing spaces over building new. (https://www.porters.org/sustainability/)
- **Other traditions:** the German (New Girl tradition), rings presented to New Girls by Old Girls at end of ninth grade, Spirit Week, Founder's Day, Daisy Days. School's ring framing: in 1843 the wedding-ring exchange was the rite of passage, but Sarah Porter believed a woman's life "could and should be much fuller if a woman had an education and understood her agency."
- **Community life:** required daily co-curricular/team-sport participation; enrichment includes "Winter Running." (https://www.porters.org/community-life/)
- **Student leadership structures:** Nova Nine, Junior Advisors, Alliance and Affinity Heads, Student Council class reps — models for eventual club-officer structure. (https://www.porters.org/community-life/)
- **Student newspaper:** *Salmagundy*, est. Oct 27, 1945; on Issuu (https://issuu.com/missportersschool) — the likeliest home of the unverified traditions survey, and a channel for publicizing the club.
- **Location / head:** 60 Main Street, Farmington, CT 06032; head of school Dr. Katherine G. Windsor, 12th head.
