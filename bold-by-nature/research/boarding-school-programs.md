## Holderness School (Plymouth, NH)

**Claim: "Out Back" is outdoor education woven into the academic curriculum, with multi-day wilderness expeditions as coursework — CONFIRMED** (nuance: a required all-school March intensive, not spread through year-round courses).

- Out Back is an 11-day backpacking expedition in the White Mountains, run since 1969; juniors travel in small faculty-led groups, snowshoeing/hiking/skiing. (https://www.holderness.org/academics/experiential-learning/special-programs/out-back)
- It sits inside "Special Programs," the experiential-learning curriculum: for 10 days each March *all* students leave the regular classroom — 9th Project Outreach (service), 10th Artward Bound (arts), 11th Out Back, 12th Capstone (professional shadowing). Curricular and effectively universal for juniors, not opt-in. (https://www.holderness.org/academics/experiential-learning/special-programs)
- Signature element: a 3-day/3-night solo midway through — students build shelter, make fire, cook alone, carrying only pencil and paper. (https://www.holderness.org/academics/experiential-learning/special-programs/out-back)
- The school marked "50 Years of Adventure Education" for Out Back. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZUqrE6mQT5k)

## Proctor Academy (Andover, NH)

**Claim: "Mountain Classroom" off-campus semester + fully integrated on-campus outdoor program — CONFIRMED.**

- Mountain Classroom since 1972: each winter and spring term, 10 students + 2 instructors spend 10 weeks traveling the American West — place-based history, literature, science plus climbing, boating, backcountry trekking, camp craft. (https://www.proctoracademy.org/off-campus-experience/mountain-classroom)
- One of several off-campus term programs (also Ocean Classroom, European Art Classroom, etc.). (https://www.proctoracademy.org/off-campus-experience)
- On-campus integration: **Wilderness Orientation** — since 1971, *every* new student begins Proctor with a 5-day/4-night backpacking trip in the White Mountains (8 students + 2 faculty), starting on Registration Day. (https://www.proctoracademy.org/off-campus-experience/wilderness-orientation)
- Proctor Outdoor Center (built 2020) is the physical home; the 2,500-acre campus/woodlands are used directly by forestry and wildlife science classes. (https://www.proctoracademy.org/explore-proctor/campus-and-facilities)

## The Athenian School (Danville, CA)

**Claim: AWE, phased independence model, "Run-In" vocabulary — CONFIRMED (core); the exact four-phase sequence PARTIALLY VERIFIED.**

- AWE (Athenian Wilderness Experience): a rigorous 26-day expeditionary backpacking course taken junior year, High Sierra (summer session) or Death Valley (spring session); a **graduation requirement**. (https://www.athenian.org/explore/signature-programs/athenian-wilderness-experience-awe — confirmed via search excerpt; page 403s to direct fetch)
- Phase structure: instructor-led technical-skills instruction (navigation, stoves, packing, shelter), a solo, and "possible independent student travel components." An explicitly named "student-led" phase was not found — treat the relay's four-phase formulation (instructor-led → student-led → solo → independent travel) as directionally right but not fully sourced. (https://jobs.naaee.org/job/athenian-wilderness-experience-instructor-1)
- The solo is three days. (school AWE page, via search excerpt)
- "Run-In" — CONFIRMED: on homecoming day students run the final ~8 miles back to campus where the whole community welcomes them, finishing through a gate inscribed with Kurt Hahn's "There's more in you than you think." (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Athenian_School ; https://patch.com/california/danville/twenty-six-day-wilderness-experience-challenges-athen9fc51241cc)

## The Storm King School (Cornwall-on-Hudson, NY)

**Claim: started as a club, expanded year-round by demand; "survival points"; JV/Varsity/Captain ranks — CONFIRMED on all points.**

- Outdoor Adventure began as a fall/winter club (inaugural hike autumn 2021, led by Coach Tim Gillett) and expanded to spring 2022 "due to popular demand." (https://sks.org/outdoor-adventure-program-climbs-to-the-next-level/)
- "Survival points" for attendance/completing events (bigger events like Catskills overnight camping worth more); season-end rank: JV at 40, Varsity at 50, All-Stars/Future Captains at 60 — counting toward school JV/Varsity letters. (https://sks.org/fall-outdoor-adventure-2021/)
- One fall season: 23 students, ~30 destinations (Breakneck Ridge, Anthony's Nose, Slide Mountain, Lake Minnewaska…), plus mountain biking, trail clearing, indoor climbing. (https://sks.org/fall-outdoor-adventure-2021/)
- Evolution worth noting: Outdoor Adventure Award launched fall 2024; "Level 2" added for 2025–26 — longer multi-day backcountry expeditions with navigation, survival, and leadership responsibilities. (https://sks.org/outdoor-adventure-program-climbs-to-the-next-level/)
- **Relevance:** the closest structural analog to Bold by Nature's starting point — a single teacher-plus-students club that grew by demand, with a points/rank system creating progression from day one.

## Phillips Exeter Academy (Exeter, NH)

**Claim: established outing club — CONFIRMED.**

- **Phillips Exeter Outing Club** — student club for off-campus hiking, camping, kayaking, canoeing. (https://exeter.edu/student-life/campus-activities/ ; https://www.facebook.com/ExeterOC/)
- Separately, **Outdoor Challenge (ODC)** is an instructional athletics/PE course: teambuilding with hiking, canoeing, cycling, ropes courses, outdoor survival education, camping skills, orienteering, on campus and at Camp Lincoln. Exeter has both a student-run club and a curricular PE-credit option. (https://exeter.edu/courses/outdoor-challenge/)

## The Hotchkiss School (Lakeville, CT)

**Claim: established outing club — CONFIRMED.**

- **Hotchkiss Outing Club** — outdoor adventure day trips: rock climbing, high ropes, skiing, hiking, camping, canoeing, rafting, caving; moonlight hikes and overnights when conditions allow. (https://www.hotchkiss.org/community-life/find-your-interests)
- **Expeditionary Leadership** — a co-curricular building wilderness skills through trail running, rock climbing, hiking, and expedition training in all weather. (https://www.hotchkiss.org/academics/co-curricular-activities)
- Distinctive asset: **FFEAT (Fairfield Farm Ecology and Adventure Team)**, based at the school's 287-acre Fairfield Farm a mile from campus — farm work plus outdoor ecology. (https://www.hotchkiss.org/our-school/our-campus/fairfield-farm)

## Northfield Mount Hermon (Gill, MA)

**Claim: established outdoor program — CONFIRMED.**

- **Outdoor Education Program (OEP)**: mission is to enrich the athletic, residential, and academic programs with adventure and instruction outdoors. Foundation is the **NMH Outdoor Team** (course code ATC 317), fall and spring terms — canoeing, mountain biking, road biking, stand-up paddleboarding; **Winter Outdoor Class** (PEC 312) — fat biking, skating, snowshoeing, cross-country skiing. Fulfills the athletics co-curricular requirement. (https://www.nmhschool.org/athletics-home/programs — course codes sourced via search excerpts; pages not directly fetchable)
- Safety/service structure worth copying: two instructors on every off-campus activity, at least one with an advanced medical cert (e.g., Wilderness First Responder); annual service via the Source to Sea Connecticut River Clean-Up. (same NMH athletics pages)

## Choate Rosemary Hall (Wallingford, CT)

**Claim: established outing club — UNVERIFIED / effectively CORRECTED.**

- Choate's own student-activities page (100+ clubs) does **not** list a named outing club, and no dedicated outdoor-adventure program appears on choate.edu athletics/afternoon-activity pages. (https://www.choate.edu/community/student-life/student-activities ; https://www.choate.edu/athletics/teams-programs)
- A third-party directory lists an "Outdoors/Ski Club" — not confirmed on the school's own site. (https://www.boardingschoolreview.com/choate-rosemary-hall-profile)
- Choate's flagship outdoor-adjacent offering is curricular: the **Environmental Immersion Program** at the Kohler Environmental Center — yearlong interdisciplinary research/fieldwork signature program. (https://www.choate.edu/academics/signature-programs/environmental-immersion-program)
- **Action for pitch materials:** don't cite Choate as having an outing club; either drop it from the peer list or cite the Kohler/Environmental Immersion Program as the (curricular) analog.
