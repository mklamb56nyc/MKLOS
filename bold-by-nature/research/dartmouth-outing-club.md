## Additional facts

- **Governance model — student-led with professional backstop.** The DOC is student-driven, but Dartmouth's Outdoor Programs Office (OPO), a professional staff office in Robinson Hall, holds administrative responsibility for the DOC, First-Year Trips, the waterfront/Ledyard Canoe Club, Moosilauke Ravine Lodge, the Second College Grant, the climbing gym, and gear rentals. This student-front / staff-backstop split is the key structural feature to copy. (https://outdoors.dartmouth.edu/opo/)
- **Scale of the operation (as of the centennial feature):** ~$2 million annual budget, ~$19 million in endowments, 14 full-time staff plus part-time/seasonal; facilities include a ski lodge, mountain lodge, ~20 cabins, boathouse, organic farm, climbing gym, and ropes courses. (https://dartmouthalumnimagazine.com/articles/100-years-outing-club)
- **Safety record under the student-led model:** In 2009 the DOC ran ~167,000 program hours (First-Year Trips serving ~1,500 students) with only 19 minor medical incidents — the article's argument for why student leadership plus rigorous training can outperform professionalized programs. (https://dartmouthalumnimagazine.com/articles/100-years-outing-club)
- **Gear library:** Dartmouth Outdoor Rentals (Robinson Hall) lends four-season backcountry gear and apparel **free to all Dartmouth undergraduates, graduate students, faculty, and staff**, first-come first-served with a Dartmouth ID. (https://outdoors.dartmouth.edu/outdoor-programs-office/dartmouth-outdoor-rentals)
- **Leader training pipeline:** OPO-facilitated leadership seminars (risk management, group dynamics, mental health) are required for **all** DOC leaders, not just Trips leaders; the DOC/OPO also provide outdoor-skills classes and medical/safety education. (https://sites.dartmouth.edu/firstyeartrips/2025/08/15/what-training-do-my-trip-leaders-have/ ; https://outdoors.dartmouth.edu/dartmouth-outing-club/about-doc)
- **Traditions worth borrowing:**
  - **"The Fifty"** — a biannual, entirely student-organized ~50-mile hike from Moosilauke Ravine Lodge to Hanover along the AT (15,000+ ft of gain, 24–32 hours), dating to Sherman Adams's 1920 trek. (https://home.dartmouth.edu/news/2023/07/doing-fifty ; https://www.thedartmouth.com/article/2025/10/thirty-three-dartmouth-students-complete-doc-fifty)
  - **Winter Carnival** — the DOC founded Dartmouth's Winter Carnival tradition. (https://dartmouthalumnimagazine.com/articles/100-years-outing-club)
  - **Ledyard Canoe Club's ~210-mile Connecticut River paddle** to the sea, an annual tradition since 1920. (https://dartmouthalumnimagazine.com/articles/100-years-outing-club)
- **Historic legitimacy anchor:** the club has maintained its AT section since 1929 — pre-dating the trail's completion — and is one of ~30 volunteer clubs coordinated by the Appalachian Trail Conservancy; adopting a trail section is a proven way for a new club to build institutional purpose. (https://www.library.dartmouth.edu/the-appalachian-trail-at-dartmouth)
- **First-Year Trips is among the largest pre-orientation programs in the country** and doubles as the DOC's recruiting funnel — introduced in 1935 explicitly "to encourage participation in the club." (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dartmouth_Outing_Club)
- **Accessibility levers:** Trips pricing ($420 in 2026) is automatically discounted up to 100% based on financial aid, and trip types deliberately span zero-experience options (museums, farm, cabin relaxing) to technical ones (climbing, paddling) — an explicit inclusion design. (https://outdoors.dartmouth.edu/first-year-trips/about-program/faqs)
