## Williams College — Williams Outing Club (WOC)

**Name & founding**
- Founded April 20, 1915, when the "Trails and Byways" committee of the Williams Good Government Club split off to form a club explicitly "modeled on the Dartmouth Outing Club"; Professor Albert Licklider, a founder of the DOC, helped establish it. In 1932 WOC's constitution was rewritten "using the Dartmouth Outing Club's constitution as a model" — the DOC lineage is documentary, not just claimed. (https://woc.williams.edu/history/)

**Size / participation**
- "The largest student organization at Williams with over 750 members" (~a third of ~2,000 students). In 2022 the student board eliminated the $10 membership fee "citing it as a financial impediment"; students now join via a form. (https://woc.williams.edu/ ; https://today.williams.edu/magazine/get-out-and-play/)

**Signature experience — WOOLF**
- WOOLF = Williams Outdoor Orientation for Living as First-years (originally "Williams Organization for Outdoor Living for Freshmen"). Club history dates it to fall 1977 (7 students disappointed with Freshmen Days; 84 freshmen the first year); the current WOOLF site and Williams magazine say 1978 — a source discrepancy to note. (https://woc.williams.edu/history/ ; https://woolf.williams.edu/)
- The oldest and most popular of several "EPHventures" orientation options — **not mandatory**, doesn't serve all incoming students. ~350 first-years (more than half of a ~550 class) in groups of 10–12. Trip types: backpacking (5–15 mi/day by ability), climbing, canoeing, trail crew, "BaseCampus" (low-intensity, sleeps in dorms), Art in Nature. "Welcomes all first-years, regardless of outdoor experience or ability." (https://today.williams.edu/magazine/get-out-and-play/ ; https://woolf.williams.edu/)
- WOOLF leaders take a week-long training course including Wilderness First Aid certification. (https://woc.williams.edu/history/)

**Structure**
- Hybrid: large student board (~24 members, activity-specific "departments" since 1973) plus a professional staff director (Ralph Townsend 1957–82; Jim Briggs; Scott Lewis 1992–2024; now Ben Oliver). (https://woc.williams.edu/history/)
- Equipment room in Paresky Center — boots, winter clothing, climbing gear, snowshoes, tents, stoves, sleeping bags, "much of which WOC members can borrow for free"; technical gear requires training. Also a 12-passenger van, bouldering barn, Nate Lowe Memorial Climbing Wall (1995), permanent ropes course in Hopkins Forest (2001). (https://today.williams.edu/magazine/get-out-and-play/)
- Cabins: long history (Dome 1924-5; Berlin Mt. 1931; Harris Memorial 1932, burned 1962; Dorland Memorial 1955, sold 1980); current: rebuilt Dorland Memorial Cabin in Hopkins Forest (1981) plus a student-built primitive shelter (1989). WOC also runs PE classes for academic credit. (https://woc.williams.edu/history/)

**Traditions / vocabulary**
- **Mountain Day** — traces to 1827; abandoned 1934, revived early '80s, spontaneous again since 2000: one October Friday the president cancels classes, chapel bells play the alma mater "The Mountains," and WOC leads hikes for all levels culminating in cider donuts and singing at Stony Ledge (~600 participants). **Note: Williams' Mountain Day is a direct analog to Porter's — an outing club as the operational engine of a beloved existing tradition. This is the strongest single precedent for the "spark → fire" strategy.** (https://woc.williams.edu/history/ ; https://today.williams.edu/magazine/get-out-and-play/)
- Winter Carnival since 1915 (first intercollegiate, 1917, vs. Colby); newsletter "Sidehill Gouger" (since 1952-3); "EPHventures"; sunrise hikes and polar plunges. (https://woc.williams.edu/history/)

**Beginner culture**
- Mission: "to make the outdoors accessible to everyone, regardless of level of experience." Deliberate beginner turn under Lewis; recent inclusion work: free programming, financial aid for fee activities, affinity outings (women-of-color hike, Minority Coalition ski day), soft activities (apple picking, slack-lining, toffee-making in snow). (https://woc.williams.edu/ ; https://today.williams.edu/magazine/get-out-and-play/)

## Colby College — Colby Outing Club (COC) and COOT

**Name & founding**
- First founded 1914 (proposed by alumni trustees Robbie G. Frye 1882, an AMC member, and William C. Crawford 1888). Activity lapsed repeatedly — nothing recorded 1915–17; suspended after the club president died in the 1922 Lambda Chi fire; revived ~1935. A separate women's Outing Club formed 1924; by 1938 the club was one of the first co-educational student groups on campus. The branding "Been outside since 1949" refers to the Ski Lodge opening, not founding. (http://colbyechonews.com/a-look-back-at-the-colby-outing-club/)

**Size / participation**
- "The largest club on campus with more than 300 members annually and a mailing list of more than 1,000. An entirely student-run organization." 100+ trips a year; "we cater our trips and gear to all levels of experience." (https://life.colby.edu/what-to-do/first-year-experience/coot/ ; https://web.colby.edu/outingclub/)

**Signature experience — COOT**
- COOT = Colby Outdoor Orientation Trips, begun 1975 (50th anniversary 2024-25). Founded by Outing Club president Mary Sue Naegele Galvin '75 and Nancy Noreen '76 with student-activities director Bruce Cummings '73 — whose pitch was initially rejected by a faculty committee as "a bad idea that had nothing to do with education"; ~20 students went on the first trip. (https://magazine.colby.edu/issue/vol-112-issue-1/a-warm-wilderness-welcome-for-new-colby-students/)
- **Mandatory** — "required of all first-year Colby students": ~630 first-years and 140 student leaders on ~70 different 3-day trips (fall 2023). Range: AT backpacking, canoe expeditions, whitewater rafting, climbing, sea kayaking on Colby-owned Allen Island, surfing, trail work with the Maine Appalachian Trail Club, challenge-course trips, and mild cabin-based "Local Exploration" trips (art, fishing, yoga) — explicitly graded by exertion and amenity level. (https://news.colby.edu/story/making-friends-and-memories-during-coot/)
- Governance nuance: COOT "is managed by administrative staff with significant collaboration and assistance from student leaders" — institutionally distinct from the student-run COC today, though it grew out of it. **Pattern: when the program became universal and mandatory, the institution took administrative ownership.** (https://life.colby.edu/what-to-do/first-year-experience/coot/)

**Structure & assets**
- Entirely student-run club; weekly Sunday meetings; gear room in the basement of Mary Low with student-staffed checkout hours. COC Cabin on Great Pond in Belgrade (~20 min from campus) for member day/overnight use, with canoes, cook-out area, fire pit. Adjacent ecosystem: Colby Mountaineering Club, Aidan's Climbing Wall, Perkins Arboretum trails. (https://web.colby.edu/outingclub/ ; https://colbyoutingclub.wordpress.com/cabin/)

**Traditions / vocabulary**
- "COOT" is campus vocabulary (you're "on your COOT"; "COOT leaders"); "cross-COOT" (groups hiking opposite directions meet mid-trail); spelling C-O-O-T with bodies on summits (incl. Katahdin); yearly COOT summaries taped to first-years' doors; decades of tongue-in-cheek trip/van logs in Special Collections; campus maple sugaring. (https://magazine.colby.edu/issue/vol-112-issue-1/a-warm-wilderness-welcome-for-new-colby-students/)

**Beginner culture**
- "It is our goal to provide everyone—from beginners to those experienced in the outdoors—with a trip well-suited to their interests and ability level." Program lead: "My favorite feedback... is 'I had no idea that I could do that.' It's a confidence booster." (https://life.colby.edu/what-to-do/first-year-experience/coot/)

## Bowdoin College — Bowdoin Outing Club (BOC)

**Name & founding**
- "Organized in 1948" per Bowdoin Library Special Collections & Archives (verified via Wayback; collection record at https://archivesspace.bowdoin.edu/repositories/2/archival_objects/103866). UNVERIFIED: an attribution that Phineas Sprague '50 "founded" it — his official obituary says only he "was president of the outing club." (https://obituaries.bowdoin.edu/phineas-sprague-50/)

**Size / participation**
- "The largest club on campus"; over 1,400 members on CampusGroups, "serving more than 1,000 students annually on some 150 excursions per year" — over half of ~1,800 students. (https://www.bowdoin.edu/outing/index.html ; https://www.bowdoin.edu/outing/about/index.html)

**Signature experience — Orientation Trips ("O-Trips")**
- First trips late summer 1982 (to the Bowdoin Scientific Station on Kent Island, Bay of Fundy); BOC took over the then-optional program by 1992; **mandatory for all incoming students since 2012.** Today all first-years choose among 54 experiences run by the BOC and the McKeen Center (service trips since 2002/2003 — sources differ by a year): day hiking, backpacking, sailing, surfing, whitewater kayaking, canoeing, mountain biking, meditation/wellness. In 2024: 42 BOC trips + ~15 McKeen. Former tradition: an all-class overnight in Farley Field House before departure (1992–2020). (https://www.bowdoin.edu/outing/orientation-trips/history-orientation-trips.html ; https://bowdoinorient.com/2024/09/13/students-and-faculty-reflect-on-the-storied-history-of-bowdoin-orientation-trips/)

**Structure**
- Hybrid: eight student officers working with professional staff (director + associate director). Peer leadership is a stated pillar — students complete leadership training (practiced on Merritt Island) before leading trips. (https://www.bowdoin.edu/outing/about/index.html)
- **Schwartz Outdoor Leadership Center (2002):** 5,300 sq ft purpose-built BOC headquarters — offices, five trip lockers, map room/classroom, commercial kitchen, central gear storage, fieldstone-fireplace common room — designed so "student leaders [can] take responsibility for all aspects of trip planning and logistical preparation... in this one location." (https://www.bowdoin.edu/outing/about/leadership-center.html)
- BOC Cabin in Monson, Maine, "just a few hundred yards off the Appalachian Trail," built by students; 40-ft climbing wall in the Buck Center "run almost entirely by students," free shoes/harnesses. Gear room: members borrow free for BOC trips and personal adventures. (https://www.bowdoin.edu/outing/areas/index.html)

**Beginner culture**
- "The Bowdoin Outing Club is for everyone." Most activities require no prior experience; all gear provided. Priorities: community through the outdoors, leadership development, access. (https://www.bowdoin.edu/outing/areas/index.html)

## Cross-cutting patterns

- **The relay's "common DNA" claim mostly holds, with one refinement:** "student leadership" everywhere means *student-led, professionally supported*, not purely student-run. Williams and Bowdoin pair student boards with paid directors and purpose-built support. Colby is the outlier both ways: the COC is "entirely student-run," but its flagship COOT is "managed by administrative staff" with student leaders.
- **"Largest club on campus" at all three** — participation between ~a third (Williams, Colby) and over half (Bowdoin) of the student body.
- **The first-year trip is the engine, but coverage differs:** Colby (1975) and Bowdoin (mandatory 2012) put the entire incoming class through 3-day small-group trips; Williams' WOOLF (1977/78) is optional but reaches more than half. All three use trained upper-class leaders and the same formula: ~8–12 first-years + 2 leaders.
- **Low barriers are engineered, not incidental:** free/near-free membership (Williams killed its $10 fee in 2022 as a "financial impediment"), free gear lending at all three, trips graded mild→strenuous, no-experience language on every page, deliberate soft/arts/wellness offerings.
- **Physical assets follow a common kit:** student-staffed gear room + climbing wall + at least one cabin.
- **Sense of place is explicit and institutional:** Williams' Mountain Day centers the alma mater "The Mountains"; COOT was created to fix Colby's failure to "celebrate its location in Maine"; Bowdoin's trips are framed as an introduction to Maine.
- **Dartmouth DNA is literal at Williams** (modeled on the DOC in 1915 by a DOC founder; constitution copied 1932). No direct documentary DOC link found for Colby or Bowdoin — mark the "Dartmouth model" framing as verified lineage for Williams, structural analogy only for the others.
- **Longevity caveat:** Colby's history shows repeated collapses and revivals — durability came from institutional anchoring (facilities, staff, orientation mandates), not continuous student enthusiasm. An argument for Phase 2 (faculty champion) and Phase 4 (official status) in Molly's plan.
