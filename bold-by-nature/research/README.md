## Best new material found (not in the relay)

- **The Farmington River through Farmington is a federally designated Wild & Scenic river** (Lower Farmington & Salmon Brook, 61.7 miles, March 2019, after a 12-year community effort). Massive pitch upgrade: "our campus sits on a nationally designated river."
- **Williams' Mountain Day is the strongest precedent for the whole strategy** — a beloved existing tradition (class-canceling, president-announced) that the outing club operationally runs. Exactly the "Mountain Day is the spark, Bold by Nature is the fire" move, proven at scale.
- **CT DEEP Youth Group Camping permit** — $33, up to 50 youth, 3 nights, at Peoples/American Legion State Forests; school-affiliated groups qualify; 1:10 adult ratio; apply ≥14 days ahead. This solves the Phase-5 first overnight logistically.
- **Colby's COOT origin story is Molly's playbook in miniature:** two students + a sympathetic staffer, initial faculty rejection ("a bad idea that had nothing to do with education"), ~20 students on the first trip, 50 years later it's mandatory for every first-year.
- **Storm King is the closest structural analog** to Bold by Nature's starting point: single-teacher club (2021) → year-round by demand → points/rank system → multi-day expeditions by 2025.
- **Porter's own sustainability assets** (three-quarters of campus natural space, student-run apiary, Earth Club) and existing outdoor-adjacent teams (crew on the river, equestrian, ski) give the club natural allies — and Earth Club's existence means positioning must differentiate (adventure/leadership vs. environmental activism).
- **Winding Trails** — a 350-acre XC-ski/outdoor center *in Farmington itself* — plus Hill-Stead's free trails walking distance from campus make zero-transport outings possible from day one.
