GLEN COVE SKATEPARK — CAST PROFILES & FIRST-CALL BRIEFS
Compiled July 2026. One profile per cast member, arranged against the playbook role each
fills (brief §4), plus a one-page call brief for each of §5's first conversations.
GROUND RULES: private individuals (friends, coaches, petitioners, benefactors) get ROLE
profiles built from our own records and their own public actions — no investigative
digging. Public officials, institutions, and funders carry verified public-record detail
(sources in 02 and the officials-verification pass). NOBODY below has been asked yet;
nothing here implies support.

- Role: instigator/quarterback, organizing BEHIND Glen Cove residents as the public face

GLEN COVE BENEFACTOR FRIENDS — [CORE / NOT YET; names still to add]

  foundations (Rauch, Greentree, Hagedorn, Claire Friedlander) via civic networks.
- What the record now shows this layer must do (sharper than before):
  • TSP requires ~30% OF PROJECT COST RAISED at application → the private layer must

    the exact structure to propose (Friends board + NYCT-LI as administrator).
- Ask sequence: (1) quiet counsel + "would you sit on a Friends board?"; (2) the design
  gift; (3) the lead/challenge gift once the city gate is in sight.

PART B — FIRST-CALL BRIEFS (the §5 conversations, one page each in miniature)
====================================================================
Format per call: WHY THEM / WHAT WE WANT / WHAT WE'RE NOT ASKING / OPEN WITH / HAVE
HANDY / LISTEN FOR / LANDMINES.

CALL 1 — FRED CLARK (do first; lowest stakes, highest orientation value)
- WHY: knows every civic player; the foundation door; a friend (practice run).
- WANT: 45 minutes; his read on the mayor/council/Rec Commission; 2–3 names for the
  benefactor circle; is he still Morgan Park chair.
- NOT ASKING: money; board membership; public support.
- OPEN WITH: "I want your read before I talk to anyone official."
- HAVE HANDY: the one-page case (kids need it / 9 parks on all of LI / it's been done
  next door for $300K–$1.25M / free forever).
- LISTEN FOR: who really moves parks decisions in Glen Cove; any history of a skatepark
  being floated before; which benefactor names light up.
- LANDMINES: don't let it become a Morgan Park siting conversation — that's settled.

CALL 2 — ALEX GREENBERG (the base)
- WHY: 1,214 people signed HIS petition; he publicly invited contact.
- WANT: his story (what worked, what stalled it); the petition's fate/list; whether he
  wants back in — possibly as co-lead.
- NOT ASKING: him to run anything alone; his list without him.
- OPEN WITH: "Your petition is why we know Glen Cove wants this. What happened?"
- HAVE HANDY: Manorhaven's Jett Gillis story (a 742-signature petition reopened a park);
  Brentwood's two petitions; what's changed since 2020 (DAC designation, our analysis,
  the precedent wave).
- LISTEN FOR: why it went dormant (that reason is our #1 risk to design around).
- LANDMINES: Tappen Beach was HIS site idea — honor it, then explain the City Stadium/DAC
  logic rather than dismissing.

CALL 3 — SHANE McGRANE (the credibility anchor)
- WHY: skater-led design is a GRANT REQUIREMENT; clinics open a second funding lane.
- WANT: his yes-in-principle to lead design input + run clinics; his read on LI skate
  scene politics; names of 2–3 other credible local skaters.
- NOT ASKING: unpaid work forever; anything public yet.
- OPEN WITH: the son makes this introduction natural — keep it a skater conversation.
- HAVE HANDY: TSP Community Activation Grant (up to $5K, ROLLING — his clinics could be
  funded THIS YEAR); the Long Beach NY design-rejection story (skaters redrew it).
- LISTEN FOR: what HE thinks the park needs (street/transition mix); whether the
  coaching operation is formal enough to contract with later.
- LANDMINES: never represent his industry contacts outward until he names them himself.

CALL 4 — LA FUERZA UNIDA (the anchor)
- WHY: the community whose kids this serves most; same DAC core; possible fiscal vehicle.
- WANT: a meeting with the ED (confirm it's Munera); their read on what downtown
  families need; interest in co-hosting listening sessions; fiscal-sponsor openness.
- NOT ASKING: endorsement yet; money; staff time.
- OPEN WITH: their mission, not our park — "what do the kids you serve do after school?"
- HAVE HANDY: Spanish-language one-pager; the access analysis (their neighborhood is
  the 100%-shut-out core); TSP's underserved definition (ethnicity + language count).
- LISTEN FOR: whether a skatepark ranks against their families' actual priorities —
  if it doesn't, better to know now.
- LANDMINES: ESL programs are paused — don't assume org capacity; a partnership that
  burdens them helps nobody.

CALL 5 — TWO/THREE BENEFACTOR FRIENDS (the founding funders)
- WHY: the private layer now GATES everything — TSP needs 30% raised; the match unlocks
  public money; the endowment answers the city.
- WANT (sequenced): counsel first ("am I crazy?"), then Friends-board interest, then —
  only when the city gate is in sight — the design gift ($40–60K, the West Bend move)
  and eventually a lead/challenge gift.
- NOT ASKING (yet): construction money.
- OPEN WITH: the golf-course contrast — seven private clubs, zero free skateparks; this
  is the give-back that's visible from downtown.
- HAVE HANDY: the full stack math ($1.25M Brentwood anchor; how $50K of design money
  attracted $500K in West Bend; Ann Arbor's endowment structure via the community
  foundation).
- LISTEN FOR: who else should be in the circle; appetite for naming/legacy.
- LANDMINES: Locust Valley optics — the ask is to FUND and open doors, with Glen Cove
  residents as the face.

CALL 6 — THE SKATEPARK PROJECT (intel + rapport, not an ask)
- WHY: funder + the field's knowledge center; they refer vetted builders; their scoring
  defines our exhibit.
- WANT: a get-acquainted call; confirm the MHI eligibility question (citywide vs
  DAC-tract); builder referrals for a City Stadium site tour; what makes LOIs win.
- NOT ASKING: money (LOI is Jan–Mar 2027; 30% raised first).
- HAVE HANDY: the DAC designation + access/provisioning analysis (their own scoring
  criteria, pre-assembled); the Brentwood/Berry Lane kinship.
- LISTEN FOR: whether Glen Cove's citywide MHI is a problem and how applicants in
  barbell-income towns have handled it.
- LANDMINES: don't oversell readiness — "readiness year, filing 2027" is the credible
  posture.

CALL 7 — ASSEMBLYMEMBER LAVINE'S OFFICE (begin the longest lead)
- WHY: the DASNY path IS the Brentwood path; relationships take cycles.
- WANT: a district-office meeting; introduce the project as a DISTRICT story (Glen Cove

- NOT ASKING: a grant commitment (nothing to fund yet); any public statement.
- OPEN WITH: the access map — his district's North Shore is the 100%-shut-out zone.

- LISTEN FOR: how he likes to be kept informed; staff contact for follow-ups; his read

- LANDMINES: election season (Nov 2026) — be scrupulously nonpartisan, and note Rhoads
