--- GOVERNANCE PATH (how a parks decision actually gets approved — mapped July 2026) ---
- STRUCTURE: strong-mayor charter; the MAYOR SITS ON AND VOTES WITH the City Council
  (mayor + 6 members = 7; FOUR votes pass a measure). No separate mayoral veto step.
- SITING a facility on existing city parkland: City Council resolution (4/7). Adding a
  rec facility is continued park use → NO state parkland-alienation act needed (that's
  for converting parkland AWAY from park use; standard NY practice — not GC-specific).
- ACCEPTING GRANTS: council resolution; grant-funded expenditures also return to council.
- BONDS have teeth: bond ordinances need a supermajority — May 2026's $4.9M capital bond
  FAILED on first vote (two abstentions wanting project detail), passed 5-0 two weeks
  later. Lesson: pre-brief every member; detail wins abstainers.
- WORKED EXAMPLE (Feb 2025, Austral Ave playground): CDA obtained a Nassau County grant
  → mayor presented → council unanimously approved the expenditures. Also Jan 2025:
  council accepted $553K Nassau County parks-upgrade grant (Stanco tennis etc.).
  PATTERN: grant (often via CDA/county) → mayor sponsors → council resolution → Parks
  executes. Our path: Tsirkas + Rec Commission blessing → CDA grant vehicle → mayor
  sponsors SITE-APPROVAL + grant-acceptance resolutions → council 4/7. TSP's required
  "written approval from the controlling agency" maps EXACTLY onto that council
  resolution — the critical-path gate has a named mechanism now.
