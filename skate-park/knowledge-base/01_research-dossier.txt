2. THE BETHPAGE SITUATION — WHY IT HELPS OUR CASE
====================================================================
- The Town of Oyster Bay's only skatepark, at Bethpage Community Park, has been closed for years due to TOXIC CONTAMINATION (not neglect, liability, or an anti-skatepark decision). Grumman/Navy used portions of the land as settling ponds / sludge beds before it was donated to the Town in 1962. [CONFIRMED]
- April 2024: ~16–24 buried chemical drums discovered; testing found hexavalent chromium (carcinogen), plus VOCs and PCBs. The Town is suing Northrop Grumman, alleging concealment. [CONFIRMED]
- Remediation ongoing; Grumman completed a 2nd cleanup phase Oct 30, 2025 (thermal). June 16, 2026: Town hired LiRo Engineers to redesign the whole park; redesign can't finish until PCBs are removed. [CONFIRMED — re-verify current rebuild timeline; still an OPEN tracker item]
- NEAREST EXISTING SKATEPARKS (corrects the old "North Shore has none" line — it's an ACCESS gap, not a literal void) [CONFIRMED]: the North Shore is NOT parkless, but the nearest options are a half-hour-plus drive in every direction — Manorhaven Beach Park (Port Washington) to the WEST, Veterans Skatepark (Northport) to the EAST — and the Town of Oyster Bay's own (Bethpage) is ~35 min and closed. Our modeling (§12) confirms the Glen Cove North Shore area is beyond a 20-min drive of ALL of them (nearest ≈ 25 min).
- An organized "Bethpage Skatepark Coalition" (Instagram @bethpageskateparkcoalition) exists, plus a "SAVE the Bethpage skatepark" petition. [CONFIRMED]
- STRATEGIC READ: The Town isn't anti-skatepark, but its skatepark attention and dollars are tied up for years. Counter to any "wait for Bethpage" response: modeling (§12) shows Bethpage's 20-min reach never touches the North Shore, so our area stays fully in the gap whether Bethpage is open or closed. Bethpage solves central Nassau; it does not solve the North Shore.
- CROSS-REF: Contaminated-site redemption is a repeated, winning skatepark story in the NY metro — see Berry Lane (Jersey City) AND Brentwood's Roberto Clemente Park (§8), both former toxic sites turned into premier community parks.

PURPOSE: convert "underserved" from adjectives into hard, defensible numbers. Two legs — PROVISIONING (how many parks the area should have) and ACCESS (who can actually reach one). This is state-of-the-art "local data showing the community is underserved" (§7) and complements the DAC designation (§3). Full pipeline, data, and exhibits preserved in the "skatepark-analysis-bundle."

METHODOLOGY (for defensibility):
- Demographics: US Census ACS 5-year 2018–2022 (tract level; Nassau FIPS 059, Suffolk 103). Boundaries: TIGER/Line 2023 (tracts, places, county subdivisions).
- Supply: OpenStreetMap (Overpass, leisure=skatepark) reconciled with web sources + known additions (Brentwood). Travel time: OpenRouteService isochrones (driving-car and cycling-regular), 20 min = 1200 s.
- Population math: AREAL APPORTIONMENT — tract population × (tract area inside a ring / total tract area). Standardized (more precise than whole-tract classification, which gave slightly higher, coarser numbers). CRS EPSG:32618.
- Populations of interest: Nassau County (059); Town of Oyster Bay (county subdivision); Glen Cove city (place); Glen Cove DAC area (tracts 517201/517202/517101).
- SUPPLY RULES: INCLUDED free public outdoor parks (~14 open on LI; Bethpage excluded as closed; Tanner/Copiague counted OPEN through its 2026 rebuild — conservative; Queens/NYC parks tested and INCLUDED). 13. WHY IT WORKS — SOCIAL / HEALTH / EQUITY EVIDENCE (backs Brief §1)
====================================================================
PURPOSE: the independent research behind the narrative's "our kids need this" case —
that a skatepark answers a real deficit, works across race/class, and that the shortage
falls hardest on lower-income kids (the equity spine that rebuts the "rich-kid amenity"
framing). Gathered July 2026; primary figures re-verified full-text this session.

- USC "BEYOND THE BOARD" (Pullias Center, USC Rossier + Annenberg; lead Neftalie
  Williams). Largest national study of skaters: 5,000+ survey responses, 120+ interviews
  across 7 cities, ages 13–25, deliberately centered on low-income/minoritized skaters —
  so the strongest independent evidence IS the equity story. FINDINGS: top reasons kids
  skate are to HAVE FUN (76%) and to GET AWAY FROM STRESS (62%); skating builds community
  and friendships across race, social class, and age; racially minoritized skaters
  reported feeling SAFER inside the skate community than in predominantly white spaces.
  Funded by The Skatepark Project. [CONFIRMED — figures re-verified July 2026]
  https://pullias.usc.edu/project/usc-skate-study/  |  https://skate.pullias.usc.edu/
- PUBLIC SKATEPARK DEVELOPMENT GUIDE (planners' reference; also the source of the
  1-per-25,000 provisioning benchmark in §12): credits skateparks with positive youth
  development, physical activity, mental resilience, belonging, improved academic
  performance, and "positive options for teens." [CONFIRMED]  https://publicskateparkguide.org/
- ACCESS / PHYSICAL-ACTIVITY EQUITY (PMC review): lower-income and BIPOC youth are less
  physically active and have less access to nearby, quality parks than wealthier peers;
  modern skateparks have skewed toward affluent, white suburbs — a national mirror of our
  local golf-course contrast (§3, §12). Anchors Brief §1a's "shortage falls hardest on
  the kids with the least." [CONFIRMED]  https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7494055/
- "THIRD PLACE" FRAME (Ray Oldenburg, *The Great Good Place*): free public commons beyond
  home and school; research holds these matter MOST for marginalized youth. Backs the
  "one of the last third places left" line in Brief §1c. [supporting frame]
- SKATEPARKS AS COMMUNITIES OF CARE (*Pedagogy, Culture & Society*, 2023): skateparks aided
  girls'/non-binary youth mental-health recovery during lockdown. Plus a peer-reviewed
  study (Sport & Exercise Psychology, 2023; 26 youth 14–17): skating fosters well-being,
  companionship, a valued sense of community. [CONFIRMED — supporting]
- LONG BEACH, CA CRIME OUTCOME — the safety-worry rebuttal (Brief §1d). Figures: within
  3 yrs of the park's 2003 opening, drug incidents −60.9% (~61%), violent crime −29.3%
