# Driggs / Crossed Arrows Lot 3 — property due-diligence

Evaluating whether to buy **Lot 3, Crossed Arrows Subdivision, Tetonia, Idaho** — a
~23.85-acre raw, permit-ready building lot in Teton County, at the foot of the Teton Range.
This directory captures the full research record and tracks the open items that gate a
go/no-go decision.

## The lot in one paragraph
A fully platted, county-approved building lot (PIN `RP021290000030`) with established
residential building rights under LA-35 zoning — one house plus a permitted detached guest
house. It is raw: no well, no septic, no structures, no water rights. The seller is
Hardwick "Hacker" Caldwell of Charleston, SC, who bought it new from the developer (the
Beard family) in 2023; four of the five lots' owners are a connected Charleston group, so
this is a known-party resale rather than an open-market listing. The entitlement — a
permit-ready homesite in a place where services are private (individual well + septic +
rural electric co-op) — is the premium over surrounding raw ag land.

## The catch on price
**Idaho is a non-disclosure state.** Actual sale prices are not public record (no transfer
tax, so no deed stamps; nothing in assessor/recorder). The assessed values you'll see
(~$13.8k, ~$37/yr tax) are *agricultural-productivity* figures, **not** market value.
Real market signal lives only in the MLS, gated to licensed members. The single best price
anchor is simply asking Caldwell what he paid the Beards in 2023.
