**Prepared:** 2026-07-20 · **Status:** planning-grade (±25%) order-of-magnitude estimate, not a bid.

## The plan being priced
A phased, one-story, simple-and-sleek, low-maintenance build — **designed as one project from
the start, built in two phases:**
- **Phase 1 — the "bunkhouse":** a 2-bedroom detached ADU (**1,500 sq ft**, the max ADU size) +
  a **3-car garage/shop (~800 sq ft)**, plus **all** the shared site infrastructure — sized for
  the *whole* property up front. Live in / use this while Phase 2 is designed and built.
- **Phase 2 — the main house:** a **3,500 sq ft** single-story home sited between the bunkhouse
  and garage. *(CC&R minimum is 3,000 sq ft per Karen Bond, so 3,500 clears it comfortably.)*
- **Systems throughout:** geothermal (ground-source) heat + cooling, grid-tied solar + battery,
  private well, private septic. Everything one story.

> **Big picture: ≈ $3.5M–5.1M all-in for the full build-out, ~$4.1M likely** — split roughly
> **$1.45M Phase 1** and **$2.7M Phase 2.** Land is **separate** (~$0.5M if purchased). These
> are *all-in* numbers (site + soft costs + contingency), not just sticks-and-bricks.

## Designing Phase 1 for Phase 2 (so the split costs almost nothing extra)
The point of planning both from day one is that **the expensive, disruptive site work happens
once.** Concretely, in Phase 1 you:
- **Do a single whole-site design + site plan** — position the ADU and garage to flank the
  reserved central envelope for the house, and lay the driveway to its final geometry.
- **Size the well, septic drainfield, and Fall River electrical service for the full build-out**
  (EIPH sizes septic by total bedrooms — permit the whole count now).
- **Grade the entire site once**, and **stub water/power/conduit to the future house pad** while
  the trenches are already open — so Phase 2 doesn't tear up finished driveway or landscaping.
- **Pre-size the solar inverter + electrical panel** so Phase 2 just adds panels + a battery.

This adds a small line to Phase 1 (~$12k of stub-ins + oversizing) and avoids far larger
Phase-2 rework. The main *un*avoidable phasing premium is re-mobilization and construction
inflation on the Phase-2 house (~5–10%) — the price of spreading the cash and living onsite
during the big build.

## Cost drivers you should know before reading the table
1. **This market is brutal.** Teton Valley custom is **$500–950+/sq ft** — 25–40% over Idaho
   Falls — from labor scarcity, snow-load engineering, a ~6-month build season, and altitude
   (~6,200 ft). Small structures (the ADU) cost *more* per sq ft than a big house.
2. **"Simple/sleek" is not "cheap."** Clean modern lines, flat/low-slope roofs, and big glazing
   are the **biggest cost swing** in this market ($150k–$500k on windows alone). Simple *massing*
   saves; simple *finishes* save; large glass does not.
3. **One story costs more per sq ft**, not less — more foundation and roof per sq ft of living
   space. Worth it for aging-in-place/low-maintenance, but it's a real premium.
4. **No more 30% federal tax credit.** The §25D Residential Clean Energy Credit ended 2025-12-31.
   Geothermal and solar in 2026+ are **full price** — factored in below.
5. **Septic is the wildcard.** Lowland ground + shallow water table (DD item #2) likely forces an
   **engineered/mound** system ($20–38k) rather than conventional ($10–15k). The EIPH test holes
   decide this — do them early; it swings the site budget and the building envelope.

**Update 2026-07-23 (Nevin docs):** two adjustments to the run-cost — (1) add a **~$1,000/yr
fire-pond easement fee** (Sage Creek POA fire-suppression water; likely shared across lots) — which
is also a **WUI-insurance positive**; (2) **fiber** appears available, so the internet line is fiber,
not Starlink (similar ~$100/mo, but reliable). Also note **short-term rental is effectively banned**
by the CC&Rs, so there's no offsetting rental income. Net effect on the totals below is small.

**The story:** holding the finished place runs **~$38k/yr cash (~$56k all-in with reserves)**,
dominated by **property tax, insurance, and the upkeep reserve** — all of which scale with the
*value* of what's built. The actual utility bills stay modest: well + septic mean no water/sewer
bill, and solar keeps the net electric small. Two levers unique to this property:
- **Keep the land ag-valued** (Idaho §63-604): lease the non-homesite acres for grazing/hay and
  only the ~1-ac homesite + buildings get taxed at market — *if* the CC&Rs allow ag use (verify).
  If not farmed, add ~$2–3k/yr as the full parcel goes to market value.
- **Insurance is the wildcard going up, not down** — WUI underwriting is tightening across the
  Mountain West; get a real quote early, and firewise construction/defensible space help both
  premium and insurability.

## Levers — how to move the number
- **Finish level is the master dial.** $500 vs $750/sq ft on 4,200 sq ft ≈ a **$1M** swing. A
  restrained "simple/sleek" palette (concrete/plywood/steel, modest glass) lands low; a big
  window package + luxury millwork lands high.
- **Skip or downsize the battery** (−$25–40k) if grid reliability is acceptable — though rural +
  Teton Pass isolation is a real argument *for* backup.
- **Air-source cold-climate heat pumps instead of geothermal** (−$40–60k across both phases).
  Modern cold-climate ASHPs perform well at altitude; geo wins on lifetime operating cost + the
  "minimal maintenance" goal you asked for, but the upfront gap is large now that the tax credit
  is gone.
- **Build once, not in two phases** (−~5–10% overall): one mobilization, one design cycle, no
  Phase-2 cost inflation. Phasing's upside is cash flow + living onsite during the main build.
- **The main house can't go below 3,000 sq ft** without a CC&R variance (we've sized it at
  3,500). Dropping to the 3,000 floor trims ~$0.3M; that floor is locked until we read the
  recorded covenants (DD item #3).
