## Priority / critical path
The septic determination and the price anchor gate everything else.

- [ ] **1. Price anchor — what Caldwell paid the Beards in 2023.** The only real market
      signal (Idaho non-disclosure). Get it from Caldwell directly (fastest, known party),
      or via a one-off MLS comp pull from anyone with Intermountain MLS / Teton Board of
      Realtors access, or a licensed appraiser / the title company. Near-identical lots
      sold in one window, so his number reveals the whole subdivision.
- [ ] **2. EIPH septic determination (CRITICAL PATH).** Book test holes + site evaluation
      with an EIPH Environmental Health Specialist present — needs 6–8 ft soil depth at the
      drainfield + replacement area. Lowland ground + shallow water table (5–38 ft locally)
      may force a mound/pressure system. Do this *before* house design — conventional vs.
      engineered drives budget and building envelope. EIPH septic program:
      https://eiph.id.gov/environmental-health/septic/
- [ ] **3. Pull recorded CC&Rs + Development Agreement** (filed with plat **279826**, early
      2023). Not online. Routes: title company (Schedule B), Teton County Clerk/Recorder by
      plat, or Caldwell (received at his closing). Read for: min home size, materials,
      architectural review, build deadlines, guest-house/barn/STR limits, road-maintenance
      obligations. CC&Rs can be stricter than county code and are enforced by the other lot
      owners, not the county.
- [ ] **4. Confirm subdivision is "build-ready."** Verify a **Certificate of Subdivision
      Completion** was issued and **sanitary restrictions are lifted** on the plat — the DA
      lets the County withhold building permits until improvements are accepted. Teton
      County Planning: **208-354-2593 / 208-776-8240**.
