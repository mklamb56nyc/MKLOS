# Property Investigation Dossier — Lot 3, Crossed Arrows Subdivision, Tetonia, ID

**Prepared:** July 20, 2026
**Subject:** Prospective purchase of a ~24-acre lot in the Crossed Arrows Subdivision, Teton County, Idaho
**Purpose:** Evaluate cost, development opportunity, and appeal. This dossier captures every finding, data point, and source from the research session so nothing is lost when scaffolding out in the Clough Code project.

> **Data-quality note up front:** Idaho is a **non-disclosure state**. Actual real-estate sale prices are NOT public record — they are not on deeds (Idaho has no transfer tax, so no stamps) and not in assessor/recorder records. Sale prices exist only in the MLS (Teton Board of Realtors / Intermountain MLS), accessible only to licensed members. Everything in this dossier is from open government sources (county assessor GIS, IDWR, EIPH, county LDC) except where noted. Assessed values shown are the county's modeled figures; for the ag-exempt parcels they reflect **agricultural productivity value, not market value**.

## 7. ZONING & BUILDING PERMISSIONS — LA-35, LOWLAND AGRICULTURE

Source: Teton County Land Development Code (adopted 2022-07-06, effective 2022-08-03; amended through 2024; a May 27, 2026 amended version also exists).

**Zone description:** LA-35 ("Mixed Agriculture/Wetland" in the comp plan) — average density ≤ 1 lot / 35 acres; covers lands limited by remoteness of services, topography, jurisdictional wetlands, floodplains. Intent: maintain undeveloped floodplains, protect water quality, protect native vegetation & critical wildlife habitat.

**Zoning history (clarified 2026-07-20):** the application was submitted under the OLD code, when the parcel was zoned **A/RR 2.5** (Agriculture/Rural Residential, 2.5-ac); VARD's write-up describes the applied density as **"Agriculture – 20-Acre."** Under the NEW 2022 LDC the mapped zone is now **LA-35 (Lowland Agriculture)** — "Lowland" because of the wetland/NRO overlays. The 5-lots-on-119-ac layout (~24 ac each) was approvable under the 20-ac density that governed at application; it would NOT satisfy LA-35's 35-ac density from scratch. This is why building rights are secure regardless of the label —

**Building right:** Lot 3 is a **platted lot of record** → the density question was settled at platting → **one primary dwelling, no further density hurdle**, whether you read the zone as A/RR 2.5, A-20, or LA-35.

**Home SIZE is not a county matter (confirmed 2026-07-20):** Teton County code sets **no minimum and no maximum square footage** for a primary residence — only setbacks (30/30/40) and 30-ft height. Any min/max house-size number (e.g. the **3,000–8,000 sq ft** range Karen Bond, Lot 2 owner, reports) therefore comes from the **private CC&Rs**, not zoning — see §9.

### Uses permitted by right (P) in LA-35
- Primary dwelling (house; manufactured home qualifies)
- **Detached Accessory Dwelling Unit / guest house (P in LA-35)** — main house + separate guest house allowed
- Attached ADU (P; max 1,500 sqft, 2 parking, wastewater verification)
- Accessory buildings (garage/shop); Agricultural buildings (barn/silo)
- Horse Stable / Equestrian (min 10 ac — qualifies); Backyard beekeeping; Home business; Nursery
- **Limited uses (permit required):** Short-Term Rentals (needs registration + septic-capacity verification); Bed & Breakfast

### Dimensional standards (LA-35)
- Min lot size: 1 ac; Min lot width: 100 ft
- Setbacks: **30 ft front & sides, 40 ft rear**
- Max height: **30 ft** primary & accessory; **60 ft** for true agricultural buildings

### The wetland catch (because NWI touches Lot 3's edge)
- **Wetland setback** applies — measured from the NWI boundary edge, or from an Army Corps–approved site-specific delineation. Since it's edge-touch, likely just trims a boundary strip.
- **Natural Resource Overlay (NRO) review:** if any portion of a lot is within/adjacent to the NRO, the county may require additional studies (Abbreviated Wildlife Habitat Analysis / A-WHA) before a building permit. Common work-around: site the building envelope ≥100 ft from the overlay boundary to qualify for exemption. **Placement is everything.**
- **WUI:** firewise/defensible-space building + wildfire-insurance scrutiny (but not a formal Wildfire Hazard Overlay study, since Lot 3 isn't in the mapped WHO).

## 8. BUILD PERMIT SEQUENCE FOR LOT 3 (agencies + forms)

1. **Confirm "build-ready" (Planning, 208-354-2593 / 208-776-8240):** verify the subdivision's **Required Improvements are complete** (a **Certificate of Subdivision Completion** issued) and that **sanitary restrictions are lifted** on the plat. The Development Agreement lets the County withhold building permits until improvements are accepted — a real DD gate.
2. **Soil test holes + site evaluation (EIPH):** test holes required to show 6–8 ft soil depth at the drainfield + replacement area, with an EIPH Environmental Health Specialist present. **This is the critical path** — lowland ground + shallow water table (5–38 ft locally) may force a mound/pressure system.
3. **Septic permit (EIPH):** Septic Permit Application + legal description + scaled plot plan showing lot boundaries and BOTH primary & replacement drainfield areas + structure floor plans + fee. Tank capacity = 2× daily flow; drainfield at 63 gal/person/day (IDAPA 58.01.03 + DEQ Technical Guidance Manual). Must be inspected before covering.
4. **Well:** single-family domestic well is exempt from a water-right permit, but must be drilled by a licensed driller who files a Well Driller's Report with IDWR.
5. **Grading & Erosion Control (GEC) permit + Building Permit (Teton County Building Dept):** engineer-stamped plans (Idaho-licensed structural engineer; height from grade to highest roof point); site plan showing all structures + driveway + well + septic + measurements to each property line (SAME site plan for GEC/building/septic); proof of ownership (deed); driveway/approach permit off N 3000 W or the internal road.
6. **Planning zoning check + wetland/wildlife review:** LA-35 setbacks (30/30/40), 30-ft height, wetland setback; NRO/A-WHA determination (avoid via envelope placement ≥100 ft from wetland).
7. **State + fire inspections → CO:** final electrical & plumbing inspections (Idaho Division of Building Safety); EIPH final septic approval before Certificate of Occupancy; post address per Teton County Title 13; Fire District sign-off (WUI).
8. **CC&R architectural review (private):** if the covenants establish an architectural committee, its approval is also required — separate from and potentially stricter than county code.

**Critical path takeaway:** the **septic determination drives everything** on this lowland ground — do the EIPH test holes early, before house design, because a conventional drainfield vs. an engineered system shapes both budget and building envelope.
