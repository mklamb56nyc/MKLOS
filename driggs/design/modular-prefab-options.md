# Modular / off-site construction options — achieving the High Horse Ranch look, delivered

**Question:** can we build this aesthetic off-site (factory) and deliver/assemble on the lot —
especially the **bunkhouse** — and get closer to **$400/sq ft finished** (vs. the ~$600/sq ft in
the cost model)? **Short answer: yes for the bunkhouse, with the right manufacturer and discipline;
harder for the main house.** Research July 2026 — get real quotes before relying on any number.

## The reference is literally a prefab project
High Horse Ranch was **built modular**: the modules were fabricated by **Moderna Homes** (San Jose
workshop), delivered on flatbed trucks, and craned into place — the main house from **2 primary +
11 secondary modules**, and **each guest cabin from a single module** set on **concrete piers**.
So "modular" isn't a compromise on this look — it *is* how the look was achieved. Our bunkhouse
(1,500 sf) is directly analogous to those single-/double-module guest cabins.

## Three off-site paths (and which fits)
| Path | What it is | $/sq ft (all-in, planning) | Fit for us |
|---|---|--:|---|
| **Architect-prefab (modular)** | Method, Stillwater, Dvele, Connect | ~$400–750 | Nails the look; only the value end (Method) flirts with $400 |
| **Panelized / hybrid** | Unity Homes (Bensonwood), timber-frame + SIP | ~$250–650 | Ships flat, assembled in days, high-performance; strong bunkhouse value + design control |
| **Production/regional modular + upgrades** | KIT (Caldwell ID), Mountain West Modulars (Thayne WY), Impresa, Champion | ~$150–350 base | Cheapest structure; needs an aesthetic-upgrade package to look like HHR |

## Can we hit ~$400/sq ft? — an honest read (superseded by the $500–600 local benchmark above)
- **Bunkhouse: realistic at ~$400–500/sq ft all-in, $400 being the disciplined low end.**
  - **Method Homes** quotes base **~$225–250/sq ft** (incl. finishes) and says west-coast *all-in*
    projects land **$350–500/sq ft**; it markets itself for **remote mountain sites**. In Teton
    Valley, site costs run 60–120% of the module cost, so plan ~$400–520 all-in — but $400 is in reach.
  - **Unity Homes** (panelized) all-in **$250–650/sq ft**, lower for small/simple builds; ships flat
    (cheaper haul than wide modules), Passivhaus-level airtightness (fits the low-operating-cost goal).
  - **Regional plant shell + targeted upgrades** (see strategy) can start in the low-$200s/sq ft and
    land ~$400 finished if you spend the upgrade budget only where it shows.
- **Main house: harder — plan ~$500–650/sq ft even prefab.** The full-height glass "view wall,"
  larger span, and custom layout don't shrink much in a factory, and Teton transport/site/foundation
  costs are the same. Prefab still buys schedule certainty and dodges local labor scarcity, but it
  won't drop a big-glass custom house to $400.
- **Why Teton Valley eats some of the savings:** long haul + oversize-load permits, crane, and the
  same site/foundation/utility work. Modular's real wins here are **factory labor** (vs. scarce/
  expensive local trades), **schedule certainty**, and **less exposure to the ~6-month build season**.

## How to actually reach $400/sq ft (bunkhouse playbook)
1. **Value-but-capable manufacturer** — Method, Unity (panelized), or a regional plant + upgrades.
2. **Simple form** — a clean rectangle, **1–2 modules**, flat/low-slope **shed roof** (KIT and Method
   both do shed roofs; KIT even offers 11-ft sidewalls + big sliders stock).
3. **Pier foundation** (like HHR) — cheaper than full foundation, less site disturbance, gives the
   light "floating" look.
4. **Spend the finish budget only where it shows:** the **east glass view wall**, an **exposed
   reclaimed-wood ceiling** in the main space, and **Cor-Ten** accent cladding. Keep bedrooms, bath,
   and back walls production-grade.
5. **Restraint = cheaper *and* more HHR** — concrete/sealed-slab floors, standard high-performance
   windows except the feature wall, minimal trim. The minimalist look and the budget pull the same way.
6. **Order the garage/bunkhouse as near-twins** to share design + factory setup cost.

## Delivery & assembly — how it actually lands on the lot
1. **Factory build** to ~80–90% complete (framing, cladding, windows, cabinets, fixtures, wiring,
   plumbing all in).
2. **Transport** — flatbed trucks (modules) or flat-packed (panels) to Tetonia; wide modules need
   **oversize-load permits** for the haul (reachable, ~4.5 hr from SLC / plants in ID/WY).
3. **Set** — a crane lifts modules onto the pre-built piers/foundation, often in **a single day** on
   a flat meadow lot; panelized goes up over several days.
4. **Button-up** (local crew) — marriage joints, utility hookups, **roof solar**, decks, final finish.
   **Weeks on site**, vs. 14–18 months for a site-built custom home here.
