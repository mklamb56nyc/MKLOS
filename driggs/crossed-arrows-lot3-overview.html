The Teton Pass factor read this

Everything in Jackson Hole — the town, the ski resort, and the airport (JAC) — is reached only over Teton Pass (WY-22). It's a steep two-lane mountain highway topping out at 8,431 ft with 10% grades. In good weather it's a scenic hour; in winter it's the weak link.

It closes. Winter storms and avalanche control shut it intermittently; no trailers are allowed Nov 15–Apr 1. In June 2024 a section collapsed in a landslide and the pass was fully closed for three weeks.

When it's closed, the detour is real: the only way around is south through Swan Valley & Alpine, which adds ~1 hour each way — a ~1-hour airport run becomes 2–2½ hours. Always check the pass before a Wyoming-side trip, especially to catch a flight.

Airports — the honest ranking

Idaho Falls (IDA) · ~1h 20m · 72 mi · no mountain pass
The reliable everyday choice. Open-highway drive west, plowed & dependable year-round. Direct flights to Salt Lake, Denver, Phoenix, Dallas, Seattle.
Jackson Hole (JAC) · ~1h 10m · 48 mi · over Teton Pass
Closest & most direct flights, but pass-dependent and premium-priced. Great when the pass is open; risky for tight winter connections (2h+ if it closes).
Salt Lake City (SLC) · ~4h 30m · 285 mi
Major hub — cheapest fares, most nonstops, international. The go-to for long-haul or budget trips; a half-day drive but never weather-stranded like the pass.
Bozeman, MT (BZN) is a ~3½-hour northern alternative with growing direct service.
